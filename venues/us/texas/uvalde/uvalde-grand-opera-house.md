# The Uvalde Grand Opera House

## Overview
The Uvalde Grand Opera House is one of the oldest operating opera houses in the state of Texas with the most original features still intact. Opening in 1891, the venue has been the center of Uvalde's performing arts community for over 130 years. The Opera House is volunteer-driven and serves as both a cultural venue and visitor center for the city of Uvalde.

**Location:** Uvalde, TX
**Tagline:** "Creating small town drama since 1891"

## Historical Significance

### Original Construction (1891)
- **Architect:** B. F. Trester
  - Also designed: Previous Uvalde courthouse, current Bandera, Texas courthouse
  - The Opera House was his final design before his death in February 1891
- **Commissioned by:** Uvalde Real Estate and Building Company
- **Built by:** Patrick F. Doyle
- **Construction Time:** 8 months
- **Opened:** October 1891

### The Dragon
One of the most unique and beloved features is the dragon sculpture atop the corner turret:
- Part of Richardsonian Romanesque architecture
- **Legend:** Said to be a mistakeâ€”a scribble by the architect that the builder took seriously
- **Status:** Original dragon removed due to weather damage but displayed inside; replica now on exterior turret
- Beloved symbol of the Opera House for nearly 130 years

### Historical Timeline

**1891-1908:**
- Hosted traveling musicians and vaudeville acts
- Venue for balls, dances, and graduation ceremonies
- **1908:** First United Methodist Church of Uvalde staged "The Mikado" (W.S. Gilbert operetta) - first local theatrical production

**1917:**
- Purchased by **John Nance Garner** (future U.S. Vice President) from F.A. Piper
- Garner converted corner room into his office
- Positioned desk chair in turret windows to watch over hometown
- Maintained office even while serving as VP under President Franklin D. Roosevelt

**Mid-20th Century:**
- Garner removed original stage and chairs, creating temporary office spaces
- Offices remained until early 1950s
- Auditorium stood empty for nearly two decades
- Retail stores continued on lower level

**1978:**
- Garner Estate sold Opera House to City of Uvalde for $10

**1979:**
- Commission formed to raise renovation funds
- Goal: Return Opera House to former glory

**1982:**
- Officially re-opened
- Returned as center stage of Uvalde community

**Present Day (2026):**
- Still operating as community theatre and cultural center
- 134+ years of continuous significance
- Hosts visitor center for City of Uvalde
