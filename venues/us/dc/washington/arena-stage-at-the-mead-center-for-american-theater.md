# Arena Stage at the Mead Center for American Theater

## Overview
Major regional nonprofit theater in Washington, DC. One of the nation's leading producing theaters, presenting American plays and musicals in three performance spaces at the Mead Center for American Theater. This is a producing theater company that creates its own productions, not a venue that books external acts.

## History
Arena Stage is a pioneering regional theater founded in 1950 by Zelda Fichandler, making it one of the oldest regional theaters in America. The Mead Center for American Theater opened in 2010 as a state-of-the-art facility housing three theater spaces. Known for producing American classics, new American plays, and musicals.
