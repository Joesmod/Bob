# Woolly Mammoth Theatre Company

## Overview
Tony Award-winning nonprofit theater company in Washington, DC's Penn Quarter neighborhood. For over 40 years, Woolly Mammoth has produced bold, contemporary theater that challenges audiences and develops new American plays. One of the few remaining theaters in the country to maintain a resident company of artists.

## History
Founded over 40 years ago, Woolly Mammoth has established itself as a research and development hub for American theater. The company serves as a proving ground for new plays that have gone on to productions at hundreds of theaters worldwide. Located equidistant from the US Capitol and the White House, Woolly's mission is deeply influenced by its position in the nation's capital.

**Awards & Recognition:**
- Tony Award winner
- National leader in new play development
- Plays premiered here have lasting impacts on the American theater field

**Land Acknowledgment:**
Woolly Mammoth acknowledges that the theater stands on the occupied, unceded territory of the Nacotchtank people, whose descendants belong to the Piscataway peoples. The foundation of Washington, DC and its original buildings were funded by the sale of enslaved people of African descent and built by their hands.

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 45/100 — Unlikely Fit**

While Woolly Mammoth is a prestigious and well-run theater, it's likely **not a strong lead for Opendate** for several structural reasons:

**Why They're Difficult to Convert:**

1. **Tony Award-Winning Institution**: Established theaters of this caliber typically have multi-year contracts with enterprise ticketing providers (Tessitura, PatronManager, AudienceView) that include sophisticated donor CRM, season subscriptions, and grant reporting.

2. **Nonprofit Infrastructure**: Their 501(c)(3) status requires integration with fundraising databases, grant compliance reporting, and donor cultivation tools that go beyond basic ticketing.

3. **Already Using Eventbrite**: While Eventbrite is visible for some events, a theater of this scale likely uses it only for special events/partnerships while running main programming on an enterprise system.

4. **Complex Organizational Needs**:
   - Resident company of artists (payroll/contracts)
   - New play development (rights management)
   - Educational programming
   - Rental management for 4 distinct spaces
   - Board governance and donor relations
   - Grant reporting to arts funders

5. **DC Market**: High concentration of professional theaters with established relationships to major ticketing platforms. Switching costs (financial + operational) are very high.

**Where Opendate Doesn't Compete:**
- Season subscription management for multi-show packages
- Major donor CRM with gift acknowledgment workflows
- Grant reporting and compliance dashboards
- Rights and royalties management for new plays
- Integration with major arts funding databases (NEFA, NEA, etc.)

**Potential (Small) Opportunity:**
If Opendate were to pursue this venue, the only realistic angle would be:
- **SPIT DAT Open Mic**: The longest-running open mic might use separate ticketing from the main theater. If it's independently managed or loosely affiliated, that could be a standalone opportunity.
- **Rental Spaces**: The four distinct rental spaces might use different systems than main programming. Opendate could pitch as a rental-specific tool.

**Recommendation:**
**Skip or deprioritize.** Focus Opendate's sales efforts on independent venues, small-to-mid-size theaters without enterprise contracts, and venues currently using Eventbrite/Etix/Brown Paper Tickets for their *primary* ticketing (not just special events).

Woolly Mammoth represents the type of venue Opendate would aspire to serve *after* building features for complex subscription management, donor CRM, and grant reporting. Right now, it's outside Opendate's competitive scope.

**Better Targets:** Look for community theaters, emerging performance spaces, independent music venues, and historic theaters like Brown Grand (which *is* using third-party platforms as their primary system).
