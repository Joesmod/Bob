Arizona Theatre Company is Arizona's only LORT (League of Resident Theatres) member and the Official State Theatre of Arizona. Founded in 1966 (originally as Tucson Opera Company), ATC is unique in producing full seasons in two cities—Tucson and Phoenix. Now in their 58th season, ATC creates world-class theatre about what it means to be alive today, reaching over 130,000 patrons annually through mainstage productions, student matinees, and community programs.

## History

Founded in 1966, Arizona Theatre Company has been a cornerstone of Arizona's cultural life for nearly 60 years. Originally established as a community arts organization, ATC evolved into a professional LORT theater and was designated the Official State Theatre of Arizona. The company performs at historic venues in both Tucson (Temple of Music and Art) and Phoenix (Tempe Center for the Arts), making it one of only a handful of U.S. theater companies that regularly performs in multiple cities.

## Programming & Shows

- **Genres**: Contemporary drama, classic American plays, musicals, new works
- **Season**: Full season (typically 5-6 productions per year)
- **Recent Productions**: The Roommate (directed by Marsha Mason), The Music Man, Bob & Jean: A Love Story
- **Special Programs**: National Latine Playwrights Award and Festival, student matinees ($1 tickets for 25,000 Title 1 school children annually)
- **Venues**: Temple of Music and Art (Tucson), Tempe Center for the Arts (Phoenix)

ATC produces professional, fully-staged theater with world-class artists. Each production is created in Arizona and shared with audiences in both cities.
