The Herberger Theater Center is Arizona's foremost performing arts institution, located in the heart of downtown Phoenix. Built in the late 1980s through philanthropic support from Katherine "Kax" and Bob Herberger, it was the first performing arts center of its kind in Arizona. The venue hosts three resident theater companies and presents a diverse range of productions including musicals, plays, opera, dance, and cultural events.

## History

Conceived as a nonprofit civic treasure in downtown Phoenix, the Herberger Theater was championed by attorney Richard Mallery as part of an urban revitalization effort. After a fateful lunch meeting where the Herbergers committed to the project while overlooking the future construction site, the theater opened nearly 40 years ago. It became home to several resident companies who, until the theater's opening, were performing in storefronts searching for a permanent venue.

Now led by President & CEO Mark Mettes (a 35-year veteran of the theater), the Herberger continues to play an essential role in bringing timeless classics and new works to the community.
