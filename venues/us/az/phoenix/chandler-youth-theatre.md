# Chandler Youth Theatre

## Overview
Youth theater training program and production company in Chandler/Phoenix, Arizona. Offers tuition-based multi-month programs that culminate in fully staged musical performances for friends and family. Programs include full-scale musicals, spring break camps, and summer intensives.

## History
Established youth theater program serving the greater Phoenix area. Under the leadership of renowned theater director Keith Burns, CYT has built a reputation for high-quality youth productions and professional-level training.

## Programming & Shows

### Production Structure
- **Format**: Tuition-based training programs (multi-month sessions)
- **Outcome**: Fully staged performances for friends and family
- **Rehearsals**: Twice weekly
- **Included in Tuition**: Instruction (acting, singing, movement), script, show t-shirt, direction, performance opportunities
- **Philosophy**: "No experience necessary" - inclusive programs welcoming all skill levels
- **Age Groups**: Elementary (6+), middle school (12-16), high school/summer intensives (12-18+)

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 75/100 — Good Fit**

Strong potential for Opendate, especially if family ticket sales generate meaningful revenue. Youth theater programs have unique needs that existing platforms don't serve well.

**Strengths:**
- **Dual Revenue Streams**: Tuition from participants + ticket sales to families
- **Regular Production Schedule**: Multiple shows throughout year = consistent ticketing needs
- **Built-In Audience**: Every participant has parents, grandparents, friends who buy tickets
- **High-Volume Low-Price**: Youth theater typically sells many tickets at $5-$15 range
- **Professional Leadership**: Keith Burns' reputation attracts families willing to invest
- **Multiple Performance Venues**: Working with Williams Field High School and other locations

**Current Challenges (Likely):**
- Managing participant registrations separately from audience ticket sales
- No integration between tuition collection and ticket sales
- Difficult to track which participants' families are buying tickets
- Limited customer data for marketing next session's programs

**Opendate Value Proposition:**

1. **Unified Platform**: 
   - Participant registration/tuition payment
   - Audience ticket sales
   - Cast/family communication tools

2. **Family Engagement**:
   - Automatic ticket discount codes for participating families
   - Reserved seating for immediate family of performers
   - "Share with your family" tools to boost ticket sales

3. **Marketing Automation**:
   - Email previous participants about new session auditions
   - Ticket buyers get invited to next production announcements
   - Grandparents who bought tickets get special "Grandparents Discount" for next show

4. **Reporting**:
   - Track ticket sales by cast member (helpful for families and for understanding pull)
   - Understand retention rates (how many participants return for next session)
   - Revenue breakdown: tuition vs. ticket sales

5. **Cast-Specific Features**:
   - Generate unique ticket links for each cast member to share
   - Track which performer's network buys the most tickets (gamification for kids)
   - Post-show photo galleries and video sales integration

**Why They'd Switch:**

Youth theater programs often cobble together:
- Google Forms for registration
- PayPal/Venmo for tuition
- Eventbrite for ticket sales
- Email for communication

Opendate could offer a purpose-built platform that understands the participant/audience dual model. The fact that CYT runs 6-8 productions per year means consistent ticketing volume.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Likely using Eventbrite or ShowTix4U for audience tickets
- Google Forms or similar for participant registration
- Separate payment processing for tuition

**Booking Contact Approach:**
Reach out via website contact form. Position Opendate as "the only platform built for youth theater's unique model—where your participants are also your biggest marketing channel."

Emphasize:
- Integration of registration + ticket sales
- Tools to help kids share their show with family and friends
- Better data on participant retention and family engagement
- Per-participant tracking for ticket sales

**Risk Factors:**
- Small organization may have limited budget
- Volume depends heavily on how many tickets family audiences buy
- Partnership with Williams Field HS might complicate venue/ticketing decisions

**Overall:** Solid opportunity. Youth theater is an underserved niche with specific needs. If Opendate can build features that help programs like CYT maximize both tuition and ticket revenue while simplifying operations, it's a compelling value proposition.

**Key Questions to Ask:**
1. How many total tickets do you sell per production?
2. What's your current platform for audience ticket sales?
3. How do you handle participant registration and tuition payment?
4. Do you offer reserved seating for performer families?
5. What percentage of revenue comes from tickets vs. tuition?
