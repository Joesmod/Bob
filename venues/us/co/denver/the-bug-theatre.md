The Bug Performance and Media Art Center (also known as "The Bug Theatre" or "The Bug") is an independent, accessible performance space created by local artists for emerging and independent performers who might otherwise be unable to access a venue of this kind.

## History

The space was originally built in 1912 as a nickelodeon movie house known as the Ideal Theater. Throughout the 20th century, it operated under various names including The World Theatre, The Navajo, and The Avalon. It also served as a Faith Temple Church before closing in the 1960s and being converted into an appliance storage warehouse.

In 1994, the building was purchased by local artists Reed Weimer and Chandler Romeo, who incorporated it as a federal 501(c)(3) nonprofit as "Bug Performance and Media Art Center" and named it The Bug Theatre—a tribute to the nickname neighborhood kids used to have for the space: the "Bug House."

A resident theatre company was introduced in 1998, followed by a small staff in 1999. The combined efforts of the award-winning Bug Theatre Company and dedicated administrative staff transformed it into a viable artistic entity.

## Programming & Shows

The Bug presents an eclectic mix of programming:

- **Theater**: Independent theater productions, musicals (e.g., recent production of a Canadian choir musical)
- **Comedy**: Alternative comedy shows including "The Grawlix" (Denver's premiere alt-comedy show with Adam Cayton-Holland, Andrew Orvedahl, and Ben Roy), "Bad Indian" (Native American comedy), stand-up showcases
- **Film**: Denver's independent film scene (monthly showcases since 2002)
- **Music**: Live music performances
- **Special Events**: Private events, community meetings, workshops, benefits

**Recent/Upcoming Shows (March 2026)**:
- Musical theater productions (multiple shows per weekend)
- Comedy nights including movie commentary shows
- Independent film screenings
- Storytelling events
- Educational events ("Think Discovery Channel, but live and with beer")

The venue is all-ages unless otherwise stated, with valid ID required for alcohol purchase.
