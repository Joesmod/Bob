# Pikes Peak International Hill Climb

## Overview
Annual automobile hill climb race to the summit of Pikes Peak, Colorado. Known as "The Race to the Clouds," this is America's second oldest auto race, founded in 1916. A single-day motorsports event, not a year-round venue. The 12.42-mile course climbs 4,720 feet with 156 turns from the start line (9,390 ft) to the summit (14,115 ft).

## History
Founded in 1916 by Spencer Penrose (founder of The Broadmoor Hotel, Pikes Peak Highway, and El Pomar Foundation). The view from Pikes Peak inspired Katharine Lee Bates to write "America the Beautiful" in 1893. The race has grown into an international invitational event attracting the world's best drivers and riders across multiple divisions. Featured in "Race to the Clouds: 100 Years in the Making" documentary film.

## Programming & Shows
- **Event Date**: June 21, 2026 (104th Running)
- **Type**: Invitational automobile and motorcycle hill climb
- **Divisions**: Unlimited, Time Attack, Pikes Peak GT4 Trophy by Yokohama, Open Wheel, Pikes Peak Open, Exhibition
- **Frequency**: Once per year
- **Challenge**: Thin air saps power (up to 30%) and slows reflexes as competitors climb
