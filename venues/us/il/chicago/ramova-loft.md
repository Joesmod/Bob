## About

Historic 1500+ capacity live concert hall in Chicago's Bridgeport neighborhood. Originally opened in 1929 as a movie palace with Spanish-inspired architecture, the Ramova Theatre was meticulously restored and reimagined as a multi-use venue featuring a concert hall (The Loft), Off Hours Beer Co. brewery, beer garden, and restaurant. The venue reopened December 31, 2023 to bring world-class live music back to Chicago's South Side.

## History

Built in 1929, the Ramova Theatre opened as Bridgeport's premier movie palace with iconic signage and Spanish-inspired architecture. It became a neighborhood landmark for nearly a century. After decades of closure, the space was rehabilitated by husband-and-wife team Tyler and Emily Nevius and reopened December 31, 2023 as a live music venue, brewery, and community gathering space.
