# The Historic Auditorium

## Overview
Historic performing arts venue in St. Croix Falls, Wisconsin, built in 1916. Serves as the main stage for St. Croix Festival Theatre and hosts concerts, movies, community events. Recently restored and reopened after years of vacancy.

## History
Built in 1916 as St. Croix Falls Civic Auditorium for vaudeville and opera. Became a movie theater during silent film era, later adapted for "talkies." Served multiple community purposes: basketball, Red Cross efforts during WWI/Spanish Flu, library, city offices, even a sewing factory. Added to Wisconsin and National Registers of Historic Places (2006-07). Festival Theatre began producing here in 1990. Building transferred to 210 Civic, LLC in 2023; major restoration completed with Wisconsin Historical Society Historic Preservation Award.
