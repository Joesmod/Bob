## About

Historic opera house built in 1902, recently restored and reopened in 2019 by the AOH Co. Guild 501(c)(3). Small community venue in rural Amherst, Wisconsin hosting musical performances, weddings, and private gatherings. Vacant for 47 years before restoration.

## History

**Timeline:**
- **1902**: Amherst Opera House Co. formed, building opened for community entertainment
- **1930s**: Community fish fries, talent shows, dances, wedding receptions
- **1937**: Purchased by Village of Amherst for $850, WPA improvements
- **1946**: Rented to Worth Co. for fishing lure production
- **1948-1953**: Rented to Tomorrow River Schools for hot lunch program, PE classes, plays
- **1960s**: Various community events, roller skating, conservation club dinners
- **1970**: Purchased by Harrison Baker, used for storage (pipe organ collection), boarded up
- **2017**: AOH Co. Guild 501(c)(3) purchased building in August after 47 years vacant
- **2018**: 116th anniversary
- **2019**: First year officially open to the public with events and performances
- **2020**: Most public events cancelled, restoration work continued
