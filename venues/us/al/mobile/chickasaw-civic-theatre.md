Mobile area community theatre producing musicals, comedies, and dramas. Intimate venue known as "the last of the intimate theaters."

## History

Founded in 1963, Chickasaw Civic Theatre performed its first season with Our Town, Charlie's Aunt, The Mousetrap, and Sabrina Fair. The founding members nicknamed it "the tepee" because the theater moved locations several times:

- **1963**: Started in Gulf Homes auditorium
- **Early years**: Moved to old bottling plant on Highway 43
- **Then**: Relocated to former scout hut (Healy-Van Leer Building) between Paul Devine Park and Chickasaw Elementary — flooded several times
- **1973**: City of Chickasaw (Mayor J.C. Davis) moved building to Lange Drive, adjacent to City Hall
- **1996**: Renamed "Lola Phillips Playhouse" after resident director Lola Phillips (retired after 32 years)
- **2005**: Moved to current location at 801 Iroquois St.
- **2000**: Attained 501(c)(3) charitable nonprofit status

First musical was *The Boyfriend* in 1973. Since then, CCT has produced major Broadway hits including Camelot, South Pacific, The Sound of Music, Les Misérables, and A Gentleman's Guide to Love and Murder.
