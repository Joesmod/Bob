# South City Theatre, Pelham

## Overview
Award-winning 501(c)(3) nonprofit community theatre in Pelham, Alabama. Self-proclaimed "The Best Little Theatre In Alabama," SCT produces six mainstage plays annually plus youth programming. The intimate theatre moved to Pelham in 2015 and has seen triple attendance figures since relocation. Attracts approximately 3,000 audience members annually. Full facility includes performance space, rehearsal/workshop room, storage, lobby, green room, dressing rooms, and restrooms.

## History
Founded by Alan and Francie Gardner, who led the organization for 12 years through multiple facilities. In May 2012, Sue Ellen and Mike Gerrells took over leadership. Major milestone in 2015 when the board moved the theatre to Pelham, resulting in triple attendance and doubled youth programming. Over 200 productions mounted since founding. Multiple productions have represented Alabama at regional and national festivals, winning numerous awards.

## Programming & Shows
- **Mainstage**: 6 adult stage productions per season
- **Recent/Upcoming Productions**: "The Cottage" (Feb 2026), "Much Ado About Nothing," "The Shark is Broken" (July 2026), "Dial M for Murder," "Christmas Queens"
- **Youth Programs**: Acting Up workshops (ages 9-16) during school year plus two-week summer camp
- **Special Events**: Matinee & Mingle series for senior groups (Thursday matinees at $20/ticket)
- **Touring**: Some shows tour to Homewood Public Library
- **Ticket prices**: $17-$22 typical range (students/seniors $17, general $20 advance/$22 door, groups of 10+ $14)
- **Season Ticket Package**: Available

Award-winning productions include "The Gulf" (2020 SETC Best in Show), "Collected Stories" (AACTFest 2021 Outstanding Set Design), and five productions that represented Alabama at SETC: "Driving Miss Daisy," "Agnes of God," "Sylvia," "Doubt," and "'night, Mother."
