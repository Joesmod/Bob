## About

Historic event venue in Jackson Ward, Richmond's "Harlem of the South." Originally built in 1913 as vaudeville stage and movie theater. Current building constructed in late 1930s-early 1940s after fire. Beautifully restored with adjacent Speakeasy Grill restaurant in the historic Taylor Mansion (1907).

## History

**Original Building (1913)**: Built as vaudeville stage and movie theater on Second Street ("The Deuce"), the bustling hot spot of African-American entertainment in Jackson Ward.

**Golden Era**: Legendary performers included Billie Holiday, Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington, Ella Fitzgerald, James Brown, Bill "Bojangles" Robinson, B.B. King, and Cab Calloway.

**1930s Fire & Rebuild**: Original building burned down due to flammable film stock. Current structure built on same site in late 1930s-early 1940s.

**Jackson Ward Legacy**: Area known as "Black Wall Street" and "Harlem of the South" — home to Black-owned businesses, banks, insurance companies. Maggie Walker chartered first bank led by a woman here. True Reformers Bank (1888) was first bank chartered by African Americans.

**Recent Restoration**: Beautifully restored with attention to detail. Adjacent Taylor Mansion (designed by renowned Black architect John Lankford) now houses Speakeasy Grill restaurant and Speakeasy Venue (up to 200 guests).
