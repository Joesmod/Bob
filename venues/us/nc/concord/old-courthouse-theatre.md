# Old Courthouse Theatre

## Overview

Community theater in Concord, NC, housed in a beautifully converted century-old former church building. Old Courthouse Theatre (OCT) is celebrating its 51st season with five main-stage productions (three plays, two musicals), two youth productions, and special events.

## History

Old Courthouse Theatre purchased the former First Baptist Church building in the early 1980s, transforming the century-old structure into a community theater. The organization is now in its 51st season (2026), operating as a vibrant community theater that preserves Concord's small-town charm despite being the 10th largest city in North Carolina. The theater is almost entirely volunteer-run, with only one part-time business manager on staff.

## Programming & Shows

- **51st Season (2026)**: "Around the World in Five Plays"
  - The Three Musketeers (Ken Ludwig)
  - Spider's Web (Agatha Christie)
  - Annie (holiday musical)
  - Mama Won't Fly (comedy)
  - Swept Away (Avett Brothers musical — regional premiere)
- **Annual Productions**: 5 main-stage shows (3 plays, 2 musicals)
- **Youth**: 2 youth productions per year
- **Black Box**: Wilson Family Black Box Theatre with Living Room Reading Series, Post Mortem Players (resident company), 10 Minute Play Festival, Papa Capp's, monthly open mic
- **Education**: Youth program "Next Gen" with summer camp

Old Courthouse Theatre is a classic volunteer-driven community theater with only one part-time paid staff member.
