## About

The YMI Cultural Center is one of America's oldest Black cultural centers, serving the African American community since 1893. The center hosts art exhibitions, cultural programs, community gatherings, educational workshops, and performances focused on Black culture and the African Diaspora. The historic building includes an arts gallery, suites for exhibitions, and basement classrooms.

## History

Built in 1893, the Young Men's Institute (YMI) building was founded by George Vanderbilt to serve Asheville's Black community. It was Mr. Isaac Dickson and Dr. Edward Stephens' vision that users would buy the building from profits earned by stores and offices on the first floor. After much effort, the African American community paid the Vanderbilt estate $10,000 for the building in 1906.

The multi-use building was the center for social activity, supporting professional offices, a public library, and the YMI Orchestra. While the YMI flourished during segregation, integration signaled a new era and the YMI ceased to be the focal point. Following decline in the 1960s and 1970s, a coalition of nine Black churches bought the YMI in 1980. The building was restored and reestablished as the YMI Cultural Center in 1981.

## Programming & Shows

- **Art Exhibitions**: Rotating exhibitions (e.g., "Portraits of Dignity," "Art Earth & Soul Kollective")
- **Jazz & Soul Sessions**: Monthly live music series (Banjul-Banjo Series) featuring performers like Kelle Jolly and Will Boyd Project
- **Black Experience Book Club**: Monthly gatherings (4th Thursday) exploring African Diaspora literature
- **Community Programs**: Kwanzaa celebrations, Black History Month events, genealogy workshops, children's cultural labs
- **Special Events**: YMI Honors gala, Goombay Festival (annual Caribbean celebration), wedding/event expos, town halls
- **Event Rentals**: Space available for private events and community gatherings
- **Ticket Prices**: Many events free or donation-based; ticketed events typically $20-$55
