Historic tavern and live music venue in the artist community of Madrid, New Mexico. Known for great food, award-winning burgers, 12 beers on tap (New Mexican microbrews), and live music every weekend across multiple stages. Also operates the Mine Shaft Cantina (outdoor venue), Engine House Theatre, and an amphitheater for special events.

## History

Originally built during Madrid's coal mining era. In the 1950s, most of Madrid's coal mines closed and the town became a ghost town for about 20 years. Madrid has since been reborn as a vibrant artist community, and the Mine Shaft Tavern is one of its centerpieces, known for great food and live music.

## Programming & Shows

- **Live Music**: Every weekend (typically 3-5 shows per weekend)
- **Genres**: Blues, Americana, country, folk, rock, singer-songwriter, bluegrass, honkytonk, Irish folk, psychedelic blues, rockabilly, jam bands, Latin music (cumbia)
- **Frequency**: 10-15 shows per week
  - Multiple shows per day on weekends (afternoon and evening sets)
  - Thursday-Sunday regular programming
  - Open mic nights monthly
- **Venues**: 
  - Historic Mine Shaft Tavern (indoor)
  - Mine Shaft Cantina (outdoor, scenic overlook)
  - Engine House Theatre (special events)
  - Amphitheater (festivals, large events)
- **Special Events**:
  - **Crawdaddy Blues Fest**: Annual 2-day blues festival (20th anniversary in 2026, May 16-17)
  - Madrid Film Festival screenings
  - Karaoke nights
  - Bingo fundraisers
- **Ticket Prices**:
  - Most weekly shows: Free admission (no cover)
  - Crawdaddy Blues Fest: $35 advance, $40 door (per day)
