# Indian Pueblo Cultural Center Meetings, Parties & Events

## Overview
Cultural center and museum serving as the gateway to the 19 Pueblos of New Mexico. The IPCC offers meeting and event space rentals with distinct Pueblo architecture and art. The campus includes museum galleries, restaurant, retail store, and multiple event/meeting spaces. Owned and governed by the 19 Pueblos District (sovereign government formed by the Tribal Councils of the 19 New Mexico Pueblo Communities).

## History
Located on nearly 80 acres owned by the 19 Pueblos at the site of the former Albuquerque Indian School (1881-1982). The campus has evolved into a cultural and business district at the heart of Albuquerque (12th Street near Interstate 40).

## Programming & Shows
- **Cultural Events**: Traditional Pueblo dances (weekly), cultural celebrations, exhibitions
- **Dance Program**: Zuni Olla Maidens, Cellicion Traditional Dance Group, Anshe:Kwe Dance Group, multi-Pueblo groups
- **Special Events**: National Women's History Month (Pueblo Women Celebration), cultural dance performances, storytelling
- **Museum Exhibitions**: "Grounded in Clay: The Spirit of Pueblo Pottery" (Mar 2026 - Feb 2027)
- **Library Programs**: Family story time, virtual book discussions
- **Event Types**: Free cultural programming, museum admissions, private rentals
- **Rental Services**: Indoor & outdoor spaces, virtual/in-person capabilities, A/V support, catering, complimentary parking
