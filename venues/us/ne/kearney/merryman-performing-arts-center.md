Historic 768-seat performing arts center in Kearney, Nebraska, located uniquely within an active elementary school building. The Merryman hosts professional concerts, theater, comedy, family shows, and community events. Known as a vital cultural hub for south-central Nebraska.

## History

Originally built in 1926 as a junior high school, later became Central Elementary School. Renovated in 2005 to include the 768-seat Robert M. Merryman Performing Arts Center, which opened for its inaugural season in 2006-2007. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The venue features the Ron & Carol Cope Lobby and the Morris Family Theatre.

## Programming & Shows

- **Genres**: Broadway shows, concerts (country, brass, pop tribute acts), family theater, comedy, classical music
- **Frequency**: 120+ events annually
- **Annual attendance**: 40,000+ patrons
- **Season memberships**: 600+ memberships from 48 communities across central Nebraska and northern Kansas
- **Notable programming**: Morris Matinee Series (student-oriented), partnerships with Kearney Concerts Association, Kearney Symphony Orchestra, Crane River Theater Company
- **Recent acts**: Marty Stuart, Pete The Cat, CeCe Teneal, M5 Mexican Brass, Bee Gees tribute, David Burnham (Broadway)
- Events include school presentations, dance recitals, church services, weddings, and local community performances
