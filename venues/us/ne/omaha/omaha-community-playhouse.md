# Omaha Community Playhouse

## Overview
The largest community theater in the United States, founded in 1924. Two performance spaces: 558-seat Howard and Rhonda Hawks Mainstage Theatre and the 200-230-seat Howard Drew Theatre (flexible black box added in 1986). Presents 5 mainstage shows and multiple black box productions annually, each running approximately one month. Also operates extensive education and outreach programs through the Shirlee & Henry Fonda Theatre Academy.

## History
Founded in 1924 as part of the nation's Little Theatre movement. First theatre opened at 40th and Davenport in 1928. Current facility at 6915 Cass Street opened in 1959, with expansion in 1986. Nationally known for its production of "A Christmas Carol," adapted by long-time Playhouse Director Charles Jones and produced for over 40 years. Now in its second century of operation. 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Community theater - musicals, plays, classics, contemporary works
- **Mainstage**: 5 productions per year, ~1 month each, 5 performances/week
- **Howard Drew (Black Box)**: More progressive productions, ~1 month runs, 4 performances/week
- **Signature**: "A Christmas Carol" (40+ year tradition)
- **Recent/Upcoming**: Hair, Disaster!, Madagascar (youth production), various seasonal shows
- **Scale**: Hundreds of thousands of community members have attended over 100+ years
