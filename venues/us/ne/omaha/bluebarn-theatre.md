# BLUEBARN Theatre

## Overview
Professional 86-seat proscenium arch theater in Omaha's Old Market district. Founded in the late 1980s by SUNY Purchase conservatory graduates, BLUEBARN has produced over 100 plays since 1989. Moved into purpose-built theater at 10th & Pacific in 2015 after capital campaign. Mix of contemporary plays, musicals, and original works.

## History
Founded in late 1980s by theater conservatory graduates. First production: "America in Pieces" (Mamet, Grey, Shepard) with $75 budget and two white sheets in February cold. Operated from Bemis Center, then 13th Street storefront (1990-1997), then "on location" venues (Train Station, courthouse), then Old Market 86-seat theater (1998), and finally purpose-built home at 10th & Pacific (2015). 

Notable productions include original works like "Minstrel Show: The Lynching of William Brown" and "Toxic Avenger: The Musical." Toured to Washington D.C. (Vietnam Women's Memorial, 2003) and NYC Fringe Festival (2004).
