# Goodspeed Opera House

## Overview
Historic and prestigious musical theatre venue in East Haddam, CT. Built in 1876 by William H. Goodspeed, restored in 1963 after nearly being demolished. Operates under Goodspeed Musicals non-profit organization. Two venues: The Goodspeed (main stage, 400-seat Victorian theatre) and The Terris Theatre (200-seat development space in Chester, CT). Known nationally for producing and preserving American musicals, with 400+ performances annually attracting 120,000+ patrons.

## History
**Original Era (1876-1950s)**: Built as Goodspeed Opera House in 1876 by shipping/banking magnate William H. Goodspeed. Opened October 24, 1877. Housed theatre, offices, steamboat terminal, and general store. After Goodspeed's death, used as State Highway Department storage and marked for demolition in 1958.

**Restoration (1959-1963)**: Goodspeed Opera House Foundation organized in 1959 by local preservationists. Restored and rededicated June 18, 1963 with "Oh, Lady! Lady!!" Tallest wooden structure on Connecticut River.

**Expansion**: The Terris Theatre opened in 1984 in former Susan Bates knitting needle factory for new musical development. Named after actress Norma Terris (original "Magnolia" in Show Boat).

## Key Leadership
Non-profit organization governed by Board of Trustees. Employs 400+ artists and staff annually.

## Programming & Shows
- **Main Productions**: New and newly revised musicals (both venues)
- **The Goodspeed**: 400-seat Victorian theatre on 4th floor
- **The Terris Theatre**: 200-seat intimate space for new works
- **Season**: April - December, 400+ performances
- **Attendance**: 120,000+ patrons annually
- **Notable**: 50th Anniversary production of Annie (2026)
- **Special Programs**: SummerSong (week-long youth program), Gala & Auction fundraisers

## Facilities
**The Goodspeed** (6 Main St, East Haddam):
- 400-seat Victorian theatre
- Built 1876, restored 1963
- Tallest wooden structure on Connecticut River
- Raked stage with motorized winch tracks

**The Terris Theatre** (33 North Main St, Chester):
- 200-seat flexible proscenium
- Opened 1984 in former factory
- New musical development focus

**Production Facilities** (5 Commerce Drive, East Haddam):
- Chauncey Stillman Production Facility - one of largest in country
- 13,000 sq ft scene shop
- 140' x 80' paint shop
- Prop shop, costume shop (250,000+ garments from 30+ Broadway shows)
- Barrington Costume Center (costume rentals)

## Educational Programs
- Max Showalter Center for Education
- Scherer Library of Musical Theatre
- SummerSong youth program (July)
- Workshops, student performances

## Ticketing
