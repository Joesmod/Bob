# War Memorial Opera House

## Overview
Historic 3,146-seat Beaux-Arts opera house in San Francisco, California Historical Landmark. Home to San Francisco Opera and San Francisco Ballet. Designed by Arthur Brown Jr., opened October 15, 1932 with SF Opera's production of Tosca. Part of the San Francisco War Memorial & Performing Arts Center campus. Hosts 40+ opera performances annually plus SF Ballet season.

## History
Built 1932 in Beaux-Arts style by architect Arthur Brown Jr. Inaugurated October 15, 1932 with Gaetano Merola conducting Puccini's Tosca for SF Opera's opening, broadcast nationally. Served as home of SF Symphony until Davies Symphony Hall opened in 1980. Underwent significant seismic retrofit in 1996 following 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake. Addition to Franklin Street façade in 1979. Over 90 years of continuous operation as one of America's most prestigious opera houses.
