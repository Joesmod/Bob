# Castro Theatre

## Overview
The Castro Theatre is a world-renowned iconic venue and cherished landmark in San Francisco's Castro Neighborhood, the city's LGBTQ+ cultural epicenter. This designated historic landmark has been the heart of the community since 1922. Built as a movie palace, the theatre is undergoing a major $41 million renovation by Another Planet Entertainment to expand its programming capabilities while preserving its historic character.

**Location:** 429 Castro Street, San Francisco, CA 94114

## Historical Significance

### Architecture & Design
- **Built:** 1922
- **Architect:** Timothy Pfleuger (one of San Francisco's most prominent architects)
- **Style:** Lavish movie palace featuring diverse architectural influences:
  - Spanish
  - Asian
  - Italian
  - Art Deco

### Interior Features
- Captivating intimate interior
- Original ceiling artwork
- Sgraffito murals
- Long-hidden historic proscenium (being restored)
- Historic chandelier (being modernized)

### Landmark Status
Designated historic landmark, serving as the cultural heart of the Castro Neighborhood and the LGBTQ+ community.

## Major Renovation (2022-Ongoing)

### Partnership
In 2022, **Another Planet Entertainment** entered into an exclusive partnership with the Theatre to execute comprehensive rehabilitation.

### Investment
$41 million renovation project

### Key Renovation Elements

**Historic Restoration:**
- Ceiling's original artwork restoration (by Evergreene Architectural Arts)
- Sgraffito murals restoration
- Historic proscenium revival
- Historic chandelier modernization
- Iconic neon blade sign and marquee revival

**New Capabilities:**
- Standing room capacity for musical performances (first time)
- Maintaining seated experience for cinema, comedy, and other events
- Flexible configuration for diverse entertainment formats

**Modern Amenities:**
- New HVAC system
- Additional restrooms
- Fully ADA accessible orchestra, stage, and dressing rooms (first time)

**Special Features:**
- World's largest and most versatile digital organ installation

**Community Partnership:**
- BuildIT (world's first LGBTQ construction advocacy organization) providing specialized trade professionals

## Programming
