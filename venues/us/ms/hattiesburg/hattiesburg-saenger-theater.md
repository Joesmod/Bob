# Hattiesburg Saenger Theater

## Overview

Historic 978-seat performing arts theater in downtown Hattiesburg, MS. Built in 1929 as a movie palace, now hosts concerts, theater, ballet, and touring shows. Managed by Hattiesburg Convention Commission. Features original 778-pipe Robert Morton Pipe Organ.

## History

Opened Thanksgiving Day 1929 as part of the Saenger Amusement Company chain operated by brothers Abe and Julian Saenger. Designed by New Orleans architect Emile Weil in Neoclassical Revival and Art Deco styles. Originally a 997-seat movie palace with admission at six cents for children. Featured air conditioning and a signature organ played between shows.

Operated as a movie theater until 1974, when ABC Interstate Theatres closed it after showing The Exorcist. Donated to City of Hattiesburg. Underwent $3.75 million renovation in 2000, restoring original grandeur. Listed on National Register of Historic Places in 1979. Recent 2022 exterior restoration of stucco and signage.

Mayor Bobby Chain personally funded restoration and reinstallation of the original Robert Morton Pipe Organ in the 1980s, one of the few remaining organs of its kind in the US.

## Programming & Shows

**Active, diverse calendar** with 3-5 shows per month including:

- **Music**: Blues, country, rock tributes, big band, jam bands (Christone "Kingfish" Ingram, Marty Stuart, moe., Glenn Miller Orchestra, That Arena Rock Show)
- **Theater & Musicals**: Broadway productions (Legally Blonde, Frozen), local theater company Picayune Theatre Co.
- **Ballet & Dance**: World Ballet Company, South Mississippi Ballet Theatre
- **Family Shows**: Sesame Street Live, Puppy Pals, K-Pop dance parties, Disney productions
- **Special Events**: Silent films with live organ accompaniment, FestivalSouth Orchestra

**Ticket prices**: $15-$45 typical range based on touring acts and local productions.
