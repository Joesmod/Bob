# TEXAS Outdoor Musical

## Overview

Historic outdoor amphitheater in Palo Duro Canyon presenting the official play of the State of Texas. Running since 1966, this summer musical tells the story of Texas Panhandle pioneers. Located in the nation's second-largest canyon, the venue features a 600-foot cliff backdrop and seats approximately 1,700.

## About

The TEXAS Outdoor Musical is presented by Producers Associates, Inc., a nonprofit organization that has operated the venue for 60+ seasons. The amphitheater is carved into a natural basin in Palo Duro Canyon State Park. The production features a cast of 60+ actors, singers, and dancers performing against the dramatic canyon backdrop.

## History

- **Founded:** 1966 (60th season in 2026)
- **Origin:** Created by playwright Paul Green after correspondence with Margaret Harper about bringing Texas Panhandle history to the stage
- **Venue:** Pioneer Amphitheatre, built into Palo Duro Canyon
- **Significance:** Official play of the State of Texas
