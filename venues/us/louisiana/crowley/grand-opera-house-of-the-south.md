# Grand Opera House of the South

## Overview
Historic 501(c)(3) performing arts venue in downtown Crowley, LA. Built in 1901 with virgin Louisiana cypress, pine, and oak. Restored and reopened in 2008 after $4.5 million renovation. Primarily operates as a venue rental space for touring acts and local performances.

## History
Built in 1901 by David E. Lyons for $18,000. Originally called "The Grand," it was a vaudeville and minstrel venue that later showed silent movies and talkies. Hosted notable performers including Enrico Caruso, Babe Ruth, Clark Gable, and Huey Long. Closed for 69 years before being donated by the Gielen family in 2004. Restored by architect Donald J. Breaux and reopened in 2008 as a National Historical Landmark.
