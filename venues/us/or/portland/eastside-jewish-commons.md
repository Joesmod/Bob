# Eastside Jewish Commons

## Overview
Jewish community hub and event venue located at NE 24th & Sandy Blvd in Portland. Provides co-working space for Jewish professionals, venue rental for events/meetings/activities, and hosts diverse community programming. Mission is to create a joyful, inclusive, and inspiring Jewish community space where people can connect, learn, and grow.

## History
Established as a thriving community hub on Portland's Eastside. Partners with multiple Jewish organizations and denominations to provide a shared venue space. Acts as both presenter and host venue for wide range of Jewish programming, representing diverse denominations, connectivity, foci, opinions, beliefs, and engagement levels.

## Programming & Shows
- **Event types**: Lectures, workshops, concerts, cultural programs, religious services, classes, social gatherings
- **Frequency**: Multiple events weekly, year-round calendar
- **Notable programs**:
  - Concert series (e.g., Zoë Aqua and Transylvanian String Band - $20adv/$25 door)
  - Educational workshops (Rabbi Caryn Aviv on Jewish anxiety)
  - Klezmer jam sessions
  - Lunch & Learn series (Enter the Pardes - 5-session course, $18)
  - Film screenings (Winter Jewish Musical Series)
  - Wellness workshops (Women's Wellness Day - $15)
  - Senior mixers and social hours
  - Nu! to Nigun musical workshops (Chassidic melodies)
  - Community classes on Israel/Palestine history ($18/person)
- **Ticket prices**: Range from free/donation to $25, typically $15-20
- **Partner programming**: EJC hosts events by partner organizations (disclaims endorsement)
- **Venue rental**: Available for private events, meetings, activities
- **Capacity**: Multi-purpose space suitable for various event sizes

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 80/100 — Good Lead**

Very active community venue with consistent ticketed programming across diverse event types. High volume of events (multiple per week) with online ticketing already in use. Currently using Humanitix, a mission-driven platform, which suggests values alignment but also indicates they may prioritize community impact features.

**Pros:**
- High event frequency - dozens of ticketed events monthly
- Already using multiple ticketing platforms (Humanitix, PayPal, RegFox) - could benefit from consolidation
- Diverse programming attracts different audiences
- Acts as venue host for partner organizations (recurring rental income)
- Co-working space provides steady foot traffic
- Strong community hub model

**Cons:**
- Nonprofit/community center model may have budget constraints
- Currently using Humanitix (charity-focused platform) - may value social impact over cost
- Multiple stakeholders (partner organizations) could complicate platform adoption
- Fragmented ticketing across platforms (Humanitix, PayPal, RegFox) suggests decentralized decision-making

**Opportunity:**
The use of multiple ticketing systems (Humanitix for some events, PayPal for others, RegFox for classes) indicates lack of unified platform. Opendate could offer:
- Consolidated ticketing for all event types
- Better community engagement tools across partner organizations
- Streamlined experience for frequent attendees
- Data insights across all programming

**Approach:** Emphasize platform consolidation, community-building features, and ease of use for multiple partner organizations using the space.
