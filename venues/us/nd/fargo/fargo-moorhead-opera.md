## About

Fargo-Moorhead Opera (FMO) is a professional opera company founded in 1968 that brings high-quality opera experiences to the region. Despite Fargo-Moorhead being the smallest city in America to sustain a full-season professional opera company, FMO presents three fully-staged opera productions per season, plus additional concerts, educational programming, and outreach activities.

FMO is an Opera America member and serves a unique geographic territory — the only Opera America company between Minneapolis and Montana (east-west) and between Omaha and Winnipeg (north-south). The company is committed to equality, equity, and diversity, providing opportunities for performers regardless of race, sex, gender, orientation, or heritage.

## History

Founded in 1968, Fargo-Moorhead Opera has served the region for over 55 years. The company established the North of Normal Chamber series in 2017-18 to bring opera to smaller, intimate venues and showcase a wider range of works from baroque to contemporary experimental productions.

The Gate City Bank Young Artists Program provides emerging opera singers with professional development opportunities, performing for over 4,000 K-12 students annually throughout the Fargo area and presenting community concerts across the region.

## Programming & Shows

**2025-2026 Season:**

1. **La Traviata** (Verdi)  
   A timeless masterpiece of love, sacrifice, and social judgment featuring Violetta, a courtesan who gives up everything for love.

2. **Amahl and the Night Visitors** (Menotti)  
   A holiday season one-act opera for all ages — a heartwarming story of a young boy, his mother, and three mysterious visitors.

3. **A Tribute to the Three Tenors**  
   A one-night concert event honoring Pavarotti, Domingo, and Carreras with arias, ballads, and ensemble pieces.

4. **Il Tabarro** (Puccini)  
   A gripping one-act masterpiece of passion and betrayal, presented in collaboration with the Fargo-Moorhead Symphony Orchestra. Completes FMO's journey through Puccini's Il Trittico.
