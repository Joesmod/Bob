## About

The Ellendale Opera House is a beautiful historic building built in 1909, celebrating its 100th anniversary in 2009. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, this three-story structure has been undergoing restoration since 1992 when the non-profit O.P.E.R.A., Inc. took over ownership from the city.

The building originally featured a 700-seat auditorium with removable chairs for dancing and other events, second-story offices for attorneys, dentists, and doctors, and a third-story balcony. The Main Street storefront once housed notable Ellendale businesses including Randall's Grocery Store, Coleman Clothing, and Coast to Coast Hardware Store.

O.P.E.R.A., Inc. is determined to bring this magnificent structure back to its grandeur and full use by the community through dedicated volunteers, grants, donations, and annual fundraising events.

## History

**1909**: Grand opening in February with plays by the Chase-Lister Theatre Company. The opera house featured:
- 700-seat auditorium (main floor with removable chairs)
- Second-story offices rented to professionals
- Third-story balcony and offices
- Basement billiards parlor
- Main Street commercial storefronts

**Early Years**: The venue hosted a wide variety of events including traveling theater productions, stage plays, musicals, lectures, readings, and community gatherings.

**Mid-Late 1900s**: The building gradually declined as tenants left. By the late 1980s, the last tenants departed and the city eventually took possession of the deteriorating structure.

**1992**: O.P.E.R.A., Inc. (Organization of People in Ellendale for Restoration of the Arts) was formed and took over ownership, beginning restoration work.

**Restoration Progress Since 1992:**
- New roof installed
- Back wall rebuilt
- ADA access added
- Main Street storefronts renovated into Lobby & Gallery space used for theater performances, art exhibits, and entertainment programs

**2009**: Celebrated 100th anniversary.

**Ongoing**: Restoration continues with support from volunteers, local/state/federal grants, donations from current and former Ellendale residents, and annual fundraising events. Funding sources include Minnesota's Arts and Cultural Heritage Fund via the Southwest Minnesota Arts Council.
