# Fremont Abbey Arts Center

## Overview
Nonprofit 501(c)3 arts center operating multiple venues around Seattle. Main venue is a beautifully restored 1914 brick church building in Upper Fremont. Hosts live music, storytelling (The Moth), contemporary dance, multi-arts collaborative performances, and community events. Capacity ~200 people at main venue. Also curates events at Ballard Homestead, Woodlawn Hall, St. Mark's Cathedral, and University Heights Center.

## History
- **Building**: Originally St. Paul's Lutheran Church, built 1914
- **Renovations**: Major seismic upgrade and restoration 2006-2008, led by Nathan Marion
- **Nonprofit**: Founded 2005 by Karen Ward (who now works on similar projects in Portland)
- **Growth**: From 1-2 events/month to 100+ events/year
- **Grand Re-opening**: March 2008
- **Ongoing**: "Greening the Abbey" environmental upgrades (insulation, LED lighting, water reduction)

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Indie/folk/acoustic music, storytelling, contemporary dance, multi-arts collaborations
- **Frequency**: 100+ events per year across all venues
- **Regular Shows**:
  - The Moth StorySLAM (monthly storytelling competition)
  - The Round (multi-artist songwriter rounds)
  - Bandit Theater (improv comedy, 4th Fridays)
  - Open Arts Mic (1st & 3rd Tuesdays)
  - Cathedrals Concert Series (at St. Mark's Cathedral)
  - Improv (3rd Tuesdays)
  - Connection Feast (social/discussion events)
  - Not Creepy Gatherings (social events)
  - Wecelium Wellness Sound Baths
- **Ticket prices**: Varies by event, typically $15-25 for ticketed shows
- **Notable**: Mix of ticketed concerts, free community events, classes, workshops

## Venue Details (Main Fremont Abbey)
- **Capacity**: ~200 people (estimate)
- **Age Policy**: All ages for most events, 21+ for some (bar w/ ID)
- **Seating**: Mix of seating and open floor depending on event type
- **Food/Beverage**: Bar service with ID at many events
- **Building**: 1914 historic brick church
- **Parking**: Street parking nearby
- **Accessibility**: Questions? Call during business hours
- **Rentals**: Available for weddings, receptions, private events (funds staffing and operations)

## Mission & Values
- **Mission**: "Curate welcoming arts & cultural experiences where people of all ages and incomes can explore creativity, enjoy beauty, grow empathy and increase awareness."
- **Nonprofit Model**: Community-driven, volunteer-powered
- **Sustainability**: Low rent from Church of the Apostles (building owner), green operations, repurposed 1914 building
- **Arts Connect**: Free tickets and scholarships for low-income families and nonprofit workers
- **Inclusivity**: All ages, all incomes, all backgrounds welcome
- **Local Focus**: Emphasizes local music, visual art, spoken word, storytelling, contemporary dance

## Partnerships
Works with numerous local nonprofits and artist groups. Venue rental supports indie artists with 100% of ticket sales going to artists for artist-run events.
