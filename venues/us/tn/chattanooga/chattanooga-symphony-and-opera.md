# Chattanooga Symphony & Opera

## Overview

Professional symphony orchestra and opera company, the first combined professional resident symphony and opera organization in the United States (merged 1985). $2.5M annual budget serving the Greater Chattanooga community.

## History

**Symphony:** Founded November 5, 1933, by Melvin Margolin and young musicians from Chattanooga High School. Professional leadership began in 1938 with Dr. Arthur Plettner from Juilliard.

**Opera:** Founded February 1943 by Dr. Werner Wolff, Emmy Land Wolff (refugees from Nazi Germany), and Dorothy Hackett Ward with Il Trovatore.

**Merger:** 1985 — first and one of the only symphonic and opera organizations of its kind in the U.S.
