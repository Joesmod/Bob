# Rockport Opera House

## Overview
Historic performing arts venue in downtown Rockport on Rockport Harbor. Built in 1891 in Colonial Revival style, featuring original hand-painted canvas theater curtain over 100 years old. "One of the architectural and acoustical gems of New England." Owned by the Town of Rockport. Hosts concerts, theater, contra dances, weddings, conferences, and town meetings. Venue for Bay Chamber Concert Series.

## History
Designed by architect F.E. Gilkey, opened in 1891 as Rockport Town Hall. Served as town meeting space, library, theater, concert hall, and Y.M.C.A. (with basketball court and bowling alley). Nearly demolished in 1970s but saved by Rockport Garden Club with local support and Maine Commission of Arts and Humanities grant. Renovated and became Rockport Opera House. Listed on National Register of Historic Places in 1976 (Rockport Historic District). Major multi-million-dollar renovation in 1990s led by David Jackson and Bay Chamber Concerts founder Tom Wolf. Still hosts annual town meetings.
