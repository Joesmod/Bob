## History

**Founded 1983** as "Pensacola Chamber Opera" by a grassroots group of singers, parents, teachers, and opera enthusiasts who believed Pensacola could produce quality opera using local talent. First production: Mozart's *Così fan tutte* (in English).

**Key milestones:**
- Moved into Pensacola Opera Center for office/rehearsal space
- **2004**: Launched Artists in Residence program (young professionals touring Northwest Florida schools)
- **2004**: Recognized by Opera America as one of "fastest-growing companies in the United States"
- **25th Anniversary (2008)**: Expanded to new venues (UWF Pensacola, Okaloosa Walton College in Niceville); added 1,100 sq ft to Opera Center
- **May 2009**: Returned to newly renovated Saenger Theatre with landmark production of *Aida* (sell-out)
- **27th Season**: Produced and toured first original commissioned opera, *The Widow's Lantern*
- **28th Season**: Produced *Little Women* by Mark Adamo (first contemporary American opera on mainstage)
- **2026**: Celebrating 40th Anniversary Season

**Pensacola Opera Guild** (formerly Opera League): Volunteer fundraising and support wing, integral to the company.

## Opendate Fit

**Score: 30/100 — Poor Fit / Not a Target**

**Major Challenges:**
- **Enterprise ticketing system**: Using PatronManager (Salesforce-based performing arts CRM) — industry-standard for opera companies
- **Institutional complexity**: 40-year-old professional opera company with sophisticated operations
- **Integrated CRM needs**: Ticketing + subscriptions + donations + education program registration all unified in Salesforce
- **Education program complexity**: 40,000 annual participants across multiple programs (not just ticket sales)
- **High switching costs**: Salesforce/PatronManager migration is major undertaking
- **Not Opendate's target market**: Opera companies need specialized performing arts management software (donor mgmt, season subscriptions, complex seating, education programs)

**Reality Check:**
This is a professional opera company, not a club/bar/indie venue. They need:
- Subscription management (season tickets)
- Donor/patron relationship management
- Education program enrollment
- Volunteer coordination
- Grant reporting
- Complex seating (orchestra/balcony/price zones)

**Why they won't switch:**
- PatronManager is purpose-built for opera/symphony/theatre companies
- Deep integration with Salesforce donor management
- Industry standard — board/staff familiar with it
- Migration would require board approval, months of planning, database migration, staff retraining
- Opera companies prioritize donor relationships over transaction speed

**Better targets:** Independent music venues, comedy clubs, small theatres — NOT established opera/symphony companies.
