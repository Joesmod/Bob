## About

Historic event venue and former Spanish mutual aid society building in Ybor City, Tampa. Originally founded in 1902 as a social club for Asturian immigrants. Beautiful three-story Renaissance Mediterranean-style building completed ~1914 after a fire. Features a historic theater, grand ballroom with 14 original mirrors, and multiple event spaces. Now operates as a rental venue for weddings, parties, corporate events, and performances. Listed on National Register of Historic Places (1974) and designated heritage structure in Ybor City Historic District.

## History

Founded May 1902 by 546 Asturian immigrants from Havana, Cuba as a mutual aid society providing medical benefits and social services. Branch of Centro Asturiano de La Habana (est. 1886). Original wood-frame building on 7th Avenue, moved to Palm and Nebraska in impressive building. After fire, current three-story yellow brick building designed by Bonfoey and Elliot was built for $110,000. Newspaper called it "the most beautiful building in the South."

Historic theater hosted international stars including Cuban composer Ernesto Lecuona. Featured zarzuelas (Spanish musical theater) that drew such acclaim that Spanish publication El Arte de Teatro sent a full-time writer to cover productions. Club offered gymnasium, cantina, billiards, bowling, library, educational rooms, ballroom - serving as community center "from cradle to grave" for Asturian immigrant families.
