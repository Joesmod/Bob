# Croswell Opera House

## Overview
Historic 640-seat theater in Adrian, MI. Founded in 1866, the Croswell is the oldest theater in Michigan and one of the oldest continuously operating theaters in the United States. Professional-caliber community theater staging musicals, plays, and special events year-round.

## History
**Founded**: 1866
Named after Charles M. Croswell, an Adrian resident and the 17th governor of Michigan. 

**Historical Highlights**:
- Hosted legendary performers in the 19th century: Edwin Booth, Maude Adams, Otis Skinner, Mrs. Patrick Campbell
- Hosted John Philip Sousa and important political figures like Frederick Douglass and Susan B. Anthony
- Operated as a movie theater for about 50 years in the early-to-mid 20th century
- By 1967, the building was deteriorating and faced closure
- Saved by civic-minded citizens who purchased the building and revived it as a home for live theater
- Auditorium lovingly restored to movie-palace grandeur

## Mission & Vision
**Vision**: To enrich our community as a leading cultural icon and preserve our diverse heritage.

**Mission**: Promote and perpetuate the arts. Offer professional growth for artists in an historic building. Motivate all ages to participate in cultural activities. Engage our community and connect people. Goal is to be financially viable and self-sustaining.

**Values**: Safe and inspiring environment. Enhance personal growth and promote teamwork. Value and respect each person. Encourage collaboration.
