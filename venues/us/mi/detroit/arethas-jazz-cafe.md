Once the city's best-kept secret, Aretha's Jazz Cafe is the 'keeper of the cool' in the heart of the Detroit Theater District. The space honors the Queen of Soul's wish for a central downtown venue bearing her name and carrying on her legacy of commitment to up-and-coming Detroit talent.

## History

Opened in 2006 as the Jazz Café, the venue was re-christened in 2019 as "Aretha's Jazz Café" to honor Aretha Franklin's wish for a downtown venue that would bear her name and carry on her commitment to Detroit's emerging talent.

The Café's opening benefited from excellent timing, coinciding with the early wave of Detroit's revitalization. Fifteen years on, it continues to evolve in rhythm with the city's renaissance, serving as a cultural hub that mixes and mingles diverse influences while remaining rooted in Detroit's African-American majority population.

## Programming & Shows

The Café offers a diverse sampling of sounds that make Detroit a global music city:

- **Primary Focus**: Jazz, blues, soul, rock 'n roll
- **Additional Programming**: Poetry, hip-hop, funk, periodic theatre, musical theater, dance performances
- **Special Feature**: Corps of Resident Artists—musicians who hone their craft through regular weekly appearances
- **Education**: Hosts vocal and instrumental students for culminating performances led by teaching artists who work in Detroit schools
- **Frequency**: Open 5-7 nights per week
- **National Acts**: Approximately once per month featuring artists like Tuck & Patti, Bettye LaVette, Omar Sosa & Yilian Canizares, Jon Batiste (pre-Colbert)

**Primary Focus**: Local Detroit artists, with periodic national/global talent
