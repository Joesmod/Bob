# Detroit Symphony Orchestra

## Overview
Major symphony orchestra performing at Orchestra Hall in Detroit's Midtown cultural district. Founded in 1887, one of America's most respected orchestras with an active year-round season featuring classical, pops, jazz, and educational programming.

## History
Founded in 1887, the Detroit Symphony Orchestra is one of the oldest performing arts institutions in the United States. The orchestra performs at Orchestra Hall, part of the Max M. & Marjorie S. Fisher Music Center complex. Known for innovative programming and community engagement.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Classical symphonic, chamber music, pops concerts, jazz series (Civic Jazz Live)
- **Frequency**: Year-round season with multiple performances weekly
- **Ticket prices**: $10-$68+ depending on series and seating
- **Notable programming**: 
  - Classical subscription series (Mendelssohn, Beethoven, Mahler)
  - PNC Pops Series (The Sounds of Laurel Canyon, Journey tribute)
  - Jazz concerts (Dianne Reeves, Arturo Sandoval, Christian McBride)
  - Family programming (The Little Mermaid, Tubby the Tuba)
  - Sensory-friendly performances
  - Northern Lights Festival
