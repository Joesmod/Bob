Historic 582-seat Victorian theater in downtown Cheboygan, serving as the cultural hub for Northern Michigan's Straits Area. Managed by the Cheboygan Area Arts Council, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit.

## History

Originally constructed in 1877 during Michigan's lumbering era. The building housed both civic functions and entertainment, combining City Hall, Police Department, Fire Department, and the Opera House under one roof. Twice destroyed by fires and rebuilt (1888 and 1903). After being closed for 20 years, the elegantly restored Opera House reopened in 1984. The acoustically superb Victorian theater is now listed on historic registers and remains a cornerstone of the community.
