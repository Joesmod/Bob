# Howell Opera House

## Overview
Historic opera house built in 1881, owned and operated by the Livingston Arts Council. Multi-use community arts venue in downtown Howell, MI hosting concerts, theater, art exhibits, workshops, and markets. Serves as the centerpiece of Livingston County's cultural programming with over 100 annual events.

## History
Built in 1881, the Howell Opera House has been a cultural landmark in Livingston County for over 140 years. Originally the cultural heart of downtown Howell, the building has been preserved and continues to thrive as a community gathering space. Now operated by the nonprofit Livingston Arts Council since its acquisition and restoration.

## Programming & Shows
- **Opera House Live - The Storytellers**: New 6-show series, one-hour storytelling concerts, $15 tickets
- **Acoustic Cafe**: Acoustic music series featuring regional artists, jazz, improv, magic
- **Concerts at the Courthouse**: Outdoor concert series (part of LAC programming)
- **Art Exhibits**: GOT ART student show (annual, free), rotating artist exhibitions
- **Workshops**: Paint & Pour ($40), Quilling, Watercolor classes
- **Classes**: Open Studio Figure Drawing (Tuesdays, $10 cash), Portrait Drawing (3rd Tuesday), Zumba ($10)
- **Community Events**: Mahjong sessions ($5), Cultura Connects cultural immersion events ($40)
- **Markets**: Winter Market (free), Indoor Artist Market during Howell Melon Festival (Aug)
- **Comedy & Theater**: Dinner theater, comedy shows (Heywood Banks), improv
- **Private Rentals**: Weddings, celebrations, special events
- **Ticket prices**: Free-$40 depending on event type
- **Frequency**: 100+ events annually
