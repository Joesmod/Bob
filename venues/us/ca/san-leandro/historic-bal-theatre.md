# Historic BAL Theatre

## Overview
Historic 800-seat Art Deco movie palace and performing arts theatre in San Leandro, California. Built in 1945 and opened July 1, 1946. Now operated by San Leandro Curtain Call Performing Arts (501c3 nonprofit) which hosts world-class, culturally diverse programming including tribute concerts, theatre, dance, comedy, and films.

## History
Billed as "the Bay Area's most modern theater" when it opened in 1946. Featured a replica of Grauman's Chinese Theatre sidewalk with Hollywood star hand/footprints. Grand opening included Dick Jurgens Big Band, movie star Virginia Wiedler, airplanes dropping confetti, and radio personality Dean Maddox as MC. Built in 6 months for $250,000 post-WWII.

Original owner Renny LaMarre won an $800,000 antitrust lawsuit in 1962 against United California Theater Company for monopolistic film distribution practices. Theatre has operated continuously for 80 years (since 1946).

Designed by legendary Art Deco architect Vincent G. Raney (1906-2002), who specialized in movie theatres and designed over 40 theatres including the Kuhio Theater in Honolulu (1946) and El Rey Theater in Vallejo (1949). Most of his theatres have been demolished; BAL Theatre is a rare survivor.
