# La Peña Cultural Center

## Overview
Historic cultural center in Berkeley, California, founded in 1975 as a community space inspired by Chilean "peñas" (music, food, and political gathering spaces). 50-year-old nonprofit dedicated to Latinx, Caribbean, and Indigenous diasporic communities. Hosts concerts, classes, workshops, theater, and community events. Designated sanctuary space for immigrant communities.

## History
Founded in 1975 by Latin American and Californian allies as a direct response to the 1973 military coup in Chile that overthrew Salvador Allende. Became the center of the anti-Pinochet dictatorship movement in the United States. Named after Chilean "peñas" - community gatherings centered around music, food, and culture. Features the iconic "Song of Unity" mural (originally 1978, repainted 2013) depicting Victor Jara, Cesar Chavez, Oscar Romero, Woody Guthrie, Malvina Reynolds, and Bill Wahpepah.

## Programming & Shows
- **Event Types**: Concerts, theater, dance, film screenings, panel discussions, art exhibits, fundraisers
- **Music Genres**: Latin American, Caribbean, Afro-Peruvian, Brazilian, Cuban, Mexican, Puerto Rican, folk, nueva canción
- **Classes**: Mexican son jarocho, Afro Peruvian cajon & dance, Brazilian Capoeira Angola, Puerto Rican bomba & plena, Cuban salsa/timba/danzon, flamenco, yoga
- **Recurring Events**: BAILA! Dance Party, La Cumbia Party, Las Mermeladas (family music jams), Empowering Women of Color Open Mic, When We Win Film Series
- **Notable Past Artists**: Pete Seeger, Lila Downs, Pablo Milanes, Roy Brown, Mercedes Sosa, Paco de Lucia, Taj Mahal, Holly Near, Sweet Honey in the Rock, Ana Tijoux, Eddie Palmieri, Bomba Estereo

## La Peña Community Chorus
Founded in 1978, multigenerational community chorus dedicated to social justice through music with Latin American focus.
