# La Peña Cultural Center

## Overview
Historic cultural center in Berkeley, California, founded in 1975. Nonprofit community space inspired by Chilean "peñas" (music, food, and political gathering spaces). 50-year-old institution serving Latinx, Caribbean, and Indigenous diasporic communities. Hosts concerts, theater, dance, film screenings, classes, and community activism events. Designated sanctuary space for immigrant communities. Located at 3105 Shattuck Ave, Berkeley, CA 94705.

## History
Founded in 1975 by Latin American and Californian allies as a direct response to the September 11, 1973 military coup in Chile that overthrew democratically elected socialist President Salvador Allende. At least 30,000 Chileans were killed, disappeared, tortured, or exiled. La Peña became the center of the anti-Pinochet dictatorship movement in the United States - a space for community meetings, art activism, and multicultural solidarity.

**Song of Unity Mural**: The 15-by-40-foot mural in front of La Peña includes symbols and iconic figures, most prominently Chilean folk singer Victor Jara (executed by the Chilean military junta in 1973). Also depicted: Cesar Chavez, Archbishop Oscar Romero, Woody Guthrie, Malvina Reynolds, and Bill Wahpepah. Originally painted in 1978, current version painted in 2013.

La Peña is the last remaining "peña" from this era still in existence today.

## La Peña Community Chorus
Founded in 1978, shortly after La Peña opened. Multigenerational community of singers dedicated to social justice through music with a Latin American focus.

## Mission Statement
"Rooted in the Latin American and Caribbean diaspora, La Peña Cultural Center collaborates with and unites communities by preserving and celebrating cultural traditions; producing creative new works; and nurturing global grassroots social justice movements with artists, activists and allies."

## Sanctuary Status
La Peña is a designated sanctuary space. They have internal protocols to respond to ICE/CBP raids and work to protect immigrant community members. Emergency exit plans and private spaces are available.
