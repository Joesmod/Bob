# Hammer Theatre Center

## Overview
Modern 516-seat performance venue in downtown San José, managed by San José State University. Features the Sobrato Auditorium main theatre, Hammer4 Studio black box space (120 seats), and multiple event spaces including rooftop terrace. Serves the community through diverse programming featuring student, local, and international talent.

## History
Named after former San José Mayor Susan Hammer and her husband Phil Hammer. Originally completed in 1997 via collaboration between San José Redevelopment Agency and San José Repertory Theatre Company. The Rep operated for over 17 years before SJSU reopened the theatre in March 2016 as a modern performance venue. Mission focuses on illustrating Silicon Valley's unique culture of creativity, diversity, and innovation.

## Facilities
**Sobrato Auditorium** (516 seats)
- Orchestra: 158 seats + 4 wheelchair spaces
- Parterre: 189 seats  
- Balcony: 163 seats + 2 wheelchair spaces
- Large stage with orchestra pit, 41' proscenium opening, 34' stage depth
- State-of-the-art Constellation virtual audio shell

**Hammer4 Studio** (120 seats)
- Black box performance space
- Available as rehearsal studio and meeting space

**Event Spaces**
- Curtain Call Cafe Lounge (40 people)
- Mercury News Lounge (20 people)
- Rooftop Terrace (120 people)
