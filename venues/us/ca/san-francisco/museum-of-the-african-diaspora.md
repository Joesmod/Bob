# Museum of the African Diaspora (MoAD)

## Overview
Contemporary art museum in San Francisco's Yerba Buena Arts District. Opened in 2005, celebrating 20th anniversary in 2025-2026. Focuses on art and artists of the African Diaspora. Museum admission + special programs, lectures, performances, film screenings, and community events. Temporarily closed March-September 2025 for renovations, reopening October 2025.

## History
Founded in 2005 in San Francisco's Yerba Buena Arts District. Mission: "To place the contemporary art and artists of the African Diaspora at the center of the global cultural conversation."

**2025 Milestone**: Celebrating 20th anniversary with major exhibitions and renovations. Temporarily closed March-September 2025 for interior and gallery upgrades. Reopening October 2025.

**Signature Artwork**: "The Face of the African Diaspora" - a mosaic in the elevator and staircases made of over 2,000 photos from around the world (African countries, South America, West Indies, United States). Together they form an image of "Girl From Tamale," a portrait of a young Ghanaian girl taken by African American photographer Chester Higgins Jr. in Ghana in 1975. Mosaic arranged by Robert Silvers and Runaway Technologies.

## Partnership
**Historic Joint Curatorship with SFMOMA:**
In 2023, MoAD and San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (SFMOMA) announced a joint curatorship position focused on art of the African Diaspora - a rare cross-institutional collaboration. Cornelia Stokes named inaugural holder in 2025.
