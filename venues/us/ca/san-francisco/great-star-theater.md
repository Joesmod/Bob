# Great Star Theater

## Overview
Historic 500-seat theater in the heart of San Francisco's Chinatown. Established in 1925, now operating as a 501(c)3 nonprofit. The last remaining Chinese theater in any Chinatown in the United States. Features eclectic programming from Cantonese opera to modern cabaret, comedy, circus, and film.

## History
Built in 1925, the Great Star Theater has served as a cultural hub for Chinese performing arts for a century. After extensive restoration, reopened in 2021 under new nonprofit leadership. Listed as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage site for Cantonese opera preservation. Celebrated 100th anniversary (centennial) in 2025.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Cantonese opera, circus, cabaret, comedy, film screenings, dance, magic, concerts, theatrical performances
- **Diversity focus**: LGBTQ+ programming, Asian-American culture, emerging artists
- **Frequency**: 650+ events hosted since 2021 reopening
- **Ticket prices**: $15-$50 typical, special events higher
- **Audience**: 80,000+ attendees since reopening
- **Notable programming**:
  - Traditional Cantonese opera (UNESCO heritage)
  - SF Sketchfest (annual comedy festival)
  - Champagne Cabaret Holiday Party
  - Dark Kabaret (magic, comedy, music)
  - Burlesque parodies (Empire Strips Back, Harriet Potter)
  - Film screenings (Joy Luck Club, Bruce Lee classics)
  - Community free events and cultural celebrations
