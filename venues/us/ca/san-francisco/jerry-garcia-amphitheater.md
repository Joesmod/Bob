# Jerry Garcia Amphitheater

## Overview
Outdoor Greek-style amphitheater in McLaren Park, San Francisco. 1,200-seat capacity with surrounding hillside for blanket seating. Hosts outdoor concerts and events throughout the year. Named after Grateful Dead guitarist Jerry Garcia, who grew up in the adjacent Excelsior neighborhood.

## History
Built in 1971 and originally opened October 16, 1971 with seating capacity of 1,200. Natural amphitheater formation mentioned in 1936 newspaper before development. Designed by Architect Alex Yuill-Thornton, constructed by Warner and Levikow, Inc. for approximately $220,000. Upgraded in 2000 with $1 million renovation adding toilets and accessibility features. Officially renamed "Jerry Garcia Amphitheater" by Recreation and Park Commission on July 21, 2005. Annual "Jerry Day" celebration held August 7 by Deadheads. Official dedication October 29, 2005 featured Jefferson Starship.
