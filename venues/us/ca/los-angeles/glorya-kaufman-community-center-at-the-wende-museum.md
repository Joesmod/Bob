# Glorya Kaufman Community Center at the Wende Museum

## Overview
Art museum, cultural center, and archive focused on Cold War history and contemporary art. The Glorya Kaufman Community Center serves as the event and programming hub within the museum. Located in Culver City (Los Angeles area), the Wende hosts exhibitions, workshops, film screenings, yoga, mindfulness sessions, and cultural events.

## History
The Wende Museum preserves Cold War history and brings it to life through exhibitions, scholarship, education, and community engagement. Named after the German word for "turning point," referencing the fall of the Berlin Wall.

## Programming & Shows
- **Event Types**: Art exhibitions, workshops (textile arts, Bauhaus-inspired activities, origami), yoga classes, mindfulness sessions, film screenings, cultural celebrations (Nowruz), bilingual storytimes, writing circles, movement workshops
- **Frequency**: Multiple events weekly (yoga, mindfulness, workshops)
- **Notable Programs**: Night of Ideas (all-day free event), Soviet Hippie Collection workshops, Bauhaus-inspired education programs
- **Ticket Range**: Most events appear to be free or by donation; some specialized workshops may charge
- **Current Exhibitions**: "Enrique Martínez Celaya: The Sextant" (Nov 2025 - Oct 2026), "Intersections: The Architecture of Victor Adegbite and Charles Polónyi in Ghana"
