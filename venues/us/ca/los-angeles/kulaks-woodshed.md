# Kulak's Woodshed

## Overview
Intimate songwriter showcase venue in North Hollywood, founded by Paul Kulak in 1999. Community-focused, donation-based venue operated entirely by volunteers. Known for its warm, supportive atmosphere for emerging and established songwriters.

## History
Founded in 1999 by Paul Kulak as a labor of love for the songwriting community. Operates on a unique model: volunteer-run, audience donations, free admission. Has become a legendary spot in the LA songwriter scene.

## Programming & Shows
- **Type**: Singer-songwriters, acoustic performances, student recitals
- **Frequency**: 5-7 shows per week
- **Weekly Events**:
  - Monday: Open mic (sign up at 7pm, show at 8pm)
  - 2nd Tuesday: "2fer" - two-song video showcase
  - Regular featured artists Thurs-Sun
- **Admission**: FREE / donation-based
- **Notable**: Pet-friendly, self-serve snacks/coffee, no alcohol/drugs

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 20/100 — Not a Fit**

While this is an active, beloved venue with a packed calendar, its entire business model is based on being donation-supported and volunteer-run. The founder curates shows based on community and artistic merit, not commercial viability. Occasional ticketed events use Eventbrite, but these are exceptions. Venue ethos is fundamentally incompatible with commercial ticketing platforms.
