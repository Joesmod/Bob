# FireHouse Arts & Events Center

## Overview
Multi-purpose arts venue in renovated historic 1927 Bellingham fire station in Fairhaven neighborhood. Operates as classroom, performance space, cafe, community meeting space, events space, theater, and art gallery with outdoor pocket park. Nonprofit 501(c)3 under fiscal sponsorship of Allied Arts of Whatcom County. Very active event calendar with 50+ events per month across jazz, classical, folk, comedy, theater, workshops, and community programs.

## History
Built 1927 as Bellingham fire station. Renovated into arts center. Features historic map of Fairhaven created after opening (updated through early 1960s). Nonprofit organization dedicated to "connecting the Community with the Arts." Mission includes scholarships for educational events and support for economically challenged performing arts groups.

## Programming & Shows
- **Primary venue**: Performance space with MERV 13 filtration, plus outdoor Garden Stage
- **Genres**: Jazz (major focus), classical, folk, Americana, comedy, Dixieland, theater, film screenings, sound healing, workshops
- **Flagship series**: 
  - **Jazz Center of Bellingham** (partnership): Monthly jazz series featuring national/regional artists ($20 general / $10 students). Artists include Jovino Santos Neto, George Colligan, Kelsey Mines, Jun Iida, Skerik, Brian Kirk, and more
  - **Music Mondays**: Free weekly music showcases (donation-based)
  - **Jazz Project Art of Jazz** series
- **Current highlights**: 
  - Comedian Tony Deyo (3/7, $20)
  - Local Legends: Bob Storms & Bill MacDonough - Benny Goodman/Artie Shaw tribute (3/14, $25 suggested)
  - Pearl Django (4/3, $30) - acclaimed Hot Club/gypsy jazz
  - John Prine Tribute concerts (3/26-27, $25) - proceeds to flood relief
  - Trail Running Film Festival (3/19)
  - Native Jazz Quartet, Cuarto Sueño album release
- **Frequency**: 3-5 ticketed events per week, plus free Music Mondays
- **Ticket prices**: $20-$30 typical, some free community events, some suggested donation
- **Special programs**: Documenting Grace Film Series, recitals, community sing-alongs, sound journeys, workshops
