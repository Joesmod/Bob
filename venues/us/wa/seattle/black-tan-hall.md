# Black & Tan Hall

## Overview
Cooperatively-owned cultural hub and performance venue in Seattle's Hillman City neighborhood (98118 zip code). Named after the historic Black & Tan Club that was part of Seattle's vibrant Jackson Street jazz scene from the 1920s-1960s. The venue focuses on elevating Black, Brown, and Native communities, women, LGBTQIA folks, and young people through arts programming, food, and cultural events.

## History
The original Black & Tan Club was one of the famous jazz clubs along Seattle's Jackson Street corridor during the city's early jazz scene. The venue is part of ongoing efforts to preserve and share Black history in Seattle. Black & Tan Hall developed the "Seattle Green Book Self-Guided Tour," a multimedia tour highlighting Black-owned and Black-friendly businesses from the 1920s-1960s, including hotels, restaurants, clubs, and barbershops listed in the national Green Book guide for Black travelers.

Currently undergoing renovations with plans to open as a full restaurant, music venue, and cultural space at 5608 Rainier Ave S.

## Programming & Shows
- **Mission**: Centers local artists, serves soul food, and offers event space
- **Focus**: Diverse, nourishing, and accountable culture
- **Anti-gentrification model**: Worker-owned cooperative that doesn't require capital to join
- **Community values**: Confronts racism, sexism, ableism, ageism, and gentrification
- **Cultural programming**: Arts events that give voice to marginalized communities
