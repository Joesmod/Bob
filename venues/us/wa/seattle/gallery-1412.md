# Gallery 1412

## Overview
Storefront performance venue in Seattle's Central District, run as an artist collective. Small, intimate space (capacity ~40-60) focused on experimental and avant-garde music, with occasional theater and dance performances. Grassroots, independent, non-commercial venue.

## History
Occupied the space at 1412 18th Ave since fall 2004, taking over from Polestar Music Gallery. Recently completed their 15th year (as of website content). Operates as a collective of independent artists pooling resources to maintain a space for individual projects and to provide an approachable non-commercial event space.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Experimental music, avant-garde jazz, improvisation, noise, electronic, chamber music
- **Frequency**: 2-4 shows per week typically
- **Ticket prices**: $5-$15 suggested donation, pay-what-you-can (NOTAFLOF - No One Turned Away For Lack Of Funds)
- **Notable recent acts**: Bad Luck, Cecyl Ruehlen, Dennis Rea's Reaven Trio, various experimental/improvising musicians
- Very DIY/grassroots aesthetic, emphasis on supporting local and touring experimental artists

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 70/100 — Poor Fit**
Not a good candidate for Opendate. Gallery 1412 operates as an artist collective with a deliberately non-commercial, grassroots ethos. Their pay-what-you-can donation model and DIY approach is core to their identity. They use Eventbrite selectively but prefer cash/Venmo at door. Very low ticket prices ($5-15 suggested) and small capacity mean minimal revenue potential. Their mission is explicitly "independent of state and commercial interests" — switching to a commercial ticketing platform would conflict with their values and community identity.
