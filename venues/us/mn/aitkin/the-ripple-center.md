## About

Historic multi-purpose venue in downtown Aitkin featuring the fully-restored Hodgeden Opera House with acoustic accommodations. Built in 1903 with locally-fired bricks from Mississippi River mud, the building features exposed brick walls, ornate paneling, elaborate details, lofty ceilings, and hardwood flooring. Hosts live music, theater, murder mystery dinners, talent shows, weddings, private events, and community gatherings. Also offers meeting/office space and retail storefronts within the building.

## History

Built in 1903, named after Samuel Hodgeden who constructed the original Opera House in honor of his daughter. The building's bricks were locally-fired from Mississippi mud. Served as Aitkin's cultural centerpiece for over a century. Recently fully-restored to preserve acoustic quality and historic character while serving as the region's primary performing arts venue.
