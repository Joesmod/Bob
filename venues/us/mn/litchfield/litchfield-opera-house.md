## About

The Litchfield Opera House is a historic theatre building currently undergoing restoration and preservation. Managed by the Greater Litchfield Opera House Association (GLOHAI), a 501(c)3 non-profit staffed entirely by volunteers, the venue exists to "protect, preserve, and promote the Litchfield Opera House as a community and historic resource, while generally promoting the arts."

The building has hosted traveling theater troupes, stage plays, musicals, lectures, readings, revivals, local musical performances, and community gatherings over the years. While restoration work continues, GLOHAI focuses on fundraising and maintaining the historic structure.

## History

The Litchfield Opera House has a long history of hosting arts and entertainment in the Litchfield community. Through the years, it has welcomed traveling theater troupes, musical performances, lectures, and community events of all kinds. After a period of decline, GLOHAI was formed to take over the building and lead restoration efforts to bring it back to its former glory.

Restoration funding comes from volunteer efforts, grants (including support from Minnesota's Arts and Cultural Heritage Fund via the Southwest Minnesota Arts Council), donations, and annual fundraising events.
