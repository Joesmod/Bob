# Art House North

## Overview
Multi-purpose community arts space in West St Paul's West 7th neighborhood. Focuses on cultivating creativity through concerts, artist workshops, theatrical productions, and community partnerships. Part of the Art House America network inspired by Charlie Peacock and Andi Ashworth's Nashville community.

## Venue Details
- **Address**: 793 Armstrong Avenue West, St Paul, MN 55102
- **Email**: contactarthousenorth@gmail.com
- **Capacity**: Lower level holds up to 100 for events; intimate concert setting
- **Type**: Non-profit community arts center

## History
Founded in 2011 by Sara and Troy Groves to foster "Creative Community for the Common Good." Inspired by Art House communities in Nashville and Dallas. Located in historic building serving as haven for artists and neighborhood gatherings. Mission includes promoting performing arts, producing local productions, and maintaining the property.

## Programming & Shows
- **Concert Series**: Regular ticketed concerts featuring singer-songwriters (Sandra McCracken, Cindy Morgan, Pierce Pettis, Sara Groves)
- **Artist Groups**: 
  - Visual Artists Night (monthly)
  - Open Write Night for writers (monthly)
  - A Little Light on a Dark Night (film/theater/dance, monthly)
- **Special Events**:
  - Annual Songwriter Workshop (March)
  - Live at Art House series (variety show format)
  - Bucket Brigade Theater productions
  - Annual Square Dance (free community event)
- **Frequency**: 2-4 concerts per month plus ongoing artist workshops
- **Ticket prices**: Concerts typically $20-$75; workshops $25 suggested donation
