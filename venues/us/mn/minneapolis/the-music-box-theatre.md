# The Music Box Theatre

## Overview
Historic 430-seat landmark theatre in downtown Minneapolis, originally built in 1920 as a premier silent film venue. Now owned by Wooddale Church and home to Miracle City Church, while continuing to host community events and performances.

## History
**1920**: Constructed in under 11 months as "The Loring Theatre," one of the nation's premier silent film venues. Built with finest materials including large marble floor lobby and grand staircases. Known locally as "The Marble Palace."

**1930s**: Expanded to include "talkies" (sound films) as motion pictures evolved.

**1952**: Closed as a public movie theatre due to competition from television and decline of Minneapolis streetcar system.

**1952-1982**: Purchased by private owners for Minneapolis Evangelistic Auditorium, later renamed Evangel Temple under Rev. Russel Olson.

**1980s**: Significant renovations completed by Cricket Theatre Company. Renamed "The Music Box Theatre."

**1990s**: Hosted the highly-caffeinated comedy "Triple Espresso" for nearly two decades.

**2016**: Purchased by Wooddale Church to preserve and enhance the historic property. Now home to Miracle City Church while maintaining its legacy as a performance venue.

## Venue Details
- **Address**: Intersection of 14th St. and Nicollet Ave., downtown Minneapolis
- **Capacity**: 430 seats
- **Architecture**: Historic 1920s design with marble floors, grand staircases
- **Parking**: Street parking at paid meters, metered lot at 15th St. & Nicollet Ave., covered parking at Loring Hyatt Ramp (one block away)
- **Accessibility**: Dedicated drop-off zone for handicapped and Metro Mobility
- **Transit**: Bus routes 17, 18, 10, 11, 30 run directly to theatre

## Programming & Shows
- **Current use**: Church services (Miracle City Church)
- **Community events**: Available for local, national, and international entertainment
- **Historical programming**: Long history of silent films, talkies, theatrical productions, comedy shows
