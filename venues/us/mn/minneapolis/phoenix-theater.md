# Phoenix Theater (A Project of Arts Nest)

## Overview
Black box theater in Uptown Minneapolis operated by Arts' Nest (501(c)(3) non-profit). Grassroots performing arts venue featuring cabarets, plays, improv, dance, burlesque, TTRPG live plays, and experimental theater. Multiple performance and rehearsal spaces with community café. Mission-driven organization supporting emerging artists through productions, education, and resources.

## Facilities
- **Main Stage**: 14'x28' black box theater
- **Rehearsal Rooms**: 
  - Blue (20'x32')
  - Yellow (16'x32')
  - Pink (14'x25')
- **Lobby & Sparks Café**: Spacious with café tables, lounge area, concessions
- **Additional**: Art gallery ("Plumage") in lobby with rotating exhibits for sale

## History
Recently selected to bring the Cowles Center for Dance and Performing Arts back to life in collaboration with Minneapolis. Active community theater hub in the Twin Cities.

## Programming & Shows
**Extremely diverse programming**:
- **Improv**: Improvocation (monthly showcase), Very Good Improv classes
- **TTRPG Live Plays**: "Monsters, Not Monoliths!", "Double D&D" (burlesque + D&D)
- **Cabaret**: Transcendence (trans-cabaret), Late Night Spotlight
- **Dance**: Graduation Day, Entropic Dreams (tap + contemporary)
- **Theater**: Finding Elijah, Sound Museums + Cabaret
- **Comedy**: Pedro Gonzalez, The Blackout Awards
- **Music**: Reignite (pop/soul concert), Spriggle Fight Team
- **Classes**: VGI improv training (101-401 levels)

**Frequency**: Multiple shows per week, very active calendar

**Programs**:
- **Hatch**: Education for producers, tech artists, performers
- **Fledgling**: Production assistance for developing companies
- **Kindling**: Resources directory for artists
- **Plumage**: Visual art exhibit program in lobby

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 90/100 — Hot Lead (Multi-Platform User Pain Point)**
**EXCELLENT candidate**. Currently juggling multiple ticketing platforms (Showslinger, Eventbrite, Humanitix) which creates friction for both the venue and audiences. Each show/producer chooses their own platform, leading to inconsistent experience. Would benefit enormously from unified platform. 

**Key selling points**:
- High show volume (multiple per week)
- Diverse producers/renters all needing ticketing
- Mission-driven non-profit (cost-conscious)
- Artist-support focus aligns with Opendate values
- Multiple stakeholders = multiple switching motivators

**Decision makers**: Jenna Papke (ED), Sean Dillon (Managing Director), Eric Cohen (Project Manager). Arts' Nest board likely involved in platform decisions.

**Strong value prop**: "One platform for all your shows, easier for your audience, easier for your producers, lower fees"
