Historic two-story brick building in downtown Marion, South Carolina. Built in 1892, restored in 1983, now serves as a community gathering place for cultural events, civic organizations, and public performances.

## History

Erected in 1892 as the Town Hall and Opera House. The first floor originally housed the town courtroom, jail, and fire department. The second floor was used for graduation exercises, public speaking, and theatrical productions.

From the early-to-mid 20th century, the building transitioned to private commercial use, housing businesses including Gasque Motor Company and Pee Dee Motor Company. In 1983, dedicated citizens restored the building, removing commercial alterations (including showroom windows) and returning it to its original appearance.

The building features classic-revival design with radial louvers in four gables and wooden false dormers. Much of the original wainscoting and woodwork remain on the interior.
