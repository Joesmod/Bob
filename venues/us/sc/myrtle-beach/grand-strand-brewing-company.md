# Grand Strand Brewing Company

## Overview
Brewery and live entertainment venue in downtown Myrtle Beach, SC. 13,000 sq ft historic building (1952) converted to brewery in 2021. Hosts live music, community events, trivia nights, and social gatherings. Part of Myrtle Beach's Arts & Innovation District revitalization.

## History
Built in 1952 as Edwards Five & Dime Store. Sat vacant for over a decade before being redeveloped by Mashburn Construction. Grand Strand Brewing Company opened March 26, 2021, marking a significant milestone in downtown Myrtle Beach's revitalization. The preservation and adaptive reuse of this historic building is part of the city's Arts & Innovation District initiative.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Local and regional musical acts (Suavocado mentioned), acoustic, indie, variety
- **Frequency**: 
  - "Sundays on the Plaza" - monthly (every 3rd Sunday), 4-6 PM
  - Special events - 2-4 per month
  - Weekly trivia nights
- **Ticket prices**: 
  - Many events FREE
  - Ticketed events: $10-25 range (Eventbrite)
  - Membership/social events: Dungeons & Dragons ($25 with food / $15 without), Sisterhood Social (ticketed via Eventbrite)
- **Notable events**: 
  - Sundays on the Plaza (monthly live music series)
  - St. Patrick's Day Green Fest (annual)
  - Harry Potter Trivia (themed trivia nights)
  - Grand Strand Sisterhood Social (women's networking + activities)
  - Game nights with prizes

Mix of free community programming and ticketed special events. Family-friendly atmosphere emphasized. They donate $1 to Sea Haven charity for every pint sold during certain events.

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 75/100 — Mixed Potential**

Grand Strand Brewing is an active community space with a mix of free and ticketed events. They're using Eventbrite for premium events but many events are free admission, which limits immediate ticketing revenue potential.

**Pros:**
- Active event calendar (multiple events per month)
- Community-focused with strong local partnerships
- Historic building in revitalized district (good story/brand)
- Using Eventbrite (switchable platform)
- High Google rating (strong local reputation)
- Diverse programming (music, trivia, social events, charity)

**Cons:**
- Many events are FREE (no ticketing revenue)
- Primary business is brewery/restaurant (events are secondary)
- Ticketed events are occasional, not core revenue
- May prefer free events for foot traffic/beer sales over ticket revenue
- Limited contact info publicly available

**Recommendation:** Lower priority for outreach. This is a brewery first, event venue second. They use free events to drive food/beverage sales rather than ticket revenue. Opendate would only capture a small slice of their occasional ticketed events (Sisterhood Social, themed trivia nights). Better fit for venues where ticketing is primary revenue stream. Consider for future if they expand ticketed programming significantly.
