## About

The Tri-State Rodeo is an annual PRCA (Professional Rodeo Cowboys Association) Great Lakes Circuit event held in Fort Madison, Iowa. Since 1948, the rodeo has brought professional rodeo competition and entertainment to the tri-state area every Labor Day weekend. The event runs Wednesday through Saturday and attracts thousands of visitors. The venue is named the C.E. "Eddie" Richards Arena after the founder who brought rodeo to the area.

## History

Founded in 1948 by C.E. (Eddie) Richards, the Tri-State Rodeo has been an annual tradition in Fort Madison for 78 years (as of 2026). The arena is named in honor of Richards, who established the event as a cornerstone of the PRCA Great Lakes Circuit. The rodeo has grown into a major regional event with a week of activities leading up to the main rodeo performances.
