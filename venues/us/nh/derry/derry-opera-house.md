# Derry Opera House

## Overview

Historic 350-seat opera house in downtown Derry, NH, built in 1904. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The venue is operated by the Greater Derry Arts Council as a rental facility for local performing arts groups.

## History

Located in the historic Adams Memorial Building at 29 West Broadway, the building was designed by architect George G. Adams of Lawrence, MA and completed in 1904. It's a Colonial Revival structure that originally housed municipal offices and the local library, with the theater on the upper level.

The opera house was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982. It was twice damaged by fire (1914 and 1927), requiring rebuilding of portions of its interior. A major $1.5 million renovation was completed in 2001, upgrading heating, air conditioning, electric systems, restrooms, adding a new elevator, sprinkler system, and dressing rooms. The balcony features restored theatre-style seats from the Boston Opera House.
