# Franklin Opera House Inc

## Overview

Historic community opera house in Franklin, NH, built 1893. Home to Franklin Footlight Theatre, FHS Players, and Franklin Area Children's Theatre (FACT). Currently closed for major renovations. Uses Ticketor for online ticket sales.

## History

Soldiers Memorial Hall was designed by architect William M. Butterfield of Manchester and completed in 1893. Like many town halls of the era, its design included a community gathering place known as The Opera House.

Throughout its history, the venue hosted balls, conventions, graduations, sports events, dances, film showings, musicals, plays, operas, and speeches. In the 1960s, the city partitioned sections of the stage and auditorium for city services.

In 1997, when the police department and court moved, the Franklin Opera House Restoration Committee was formed and reopened the venue. The City of Franklin owns Soldiers Memorial Hall and its Opera House.

**Current Status**: Facility is closed for major renovations. The City cannot cover all renovation costs, so Franklin Opera House Inc. is fundraising to pick up the slack.

**Strong Points**:
- Uses switchable ticketing platform (Ticketor)
- Active programming with multiple resident companies
- Community support and volunteer base
- Youth programs and educational reach

**Concerns**:
- **Currently closed for renovations** - no active events
- Community nonprofit model - limited budget
- Volunteer-run operations - may lack resources for platform migration
- Small market (Franklin, NH population ~9,000)

**Timing**: Not a current prospect until renovations complete. Could be a good small-market test case when they reopen, especially if they're upgrading systems as part of the renovation.
