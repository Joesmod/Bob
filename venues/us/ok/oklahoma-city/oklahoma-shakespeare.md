## About

Oklahoma Shakespeare is Oklahoma's home for classical theatre, offering year-round programming from their location in the historic Paseo Arts District of Oklahoma City. The company operates two distinct venues:

- **Shakespeare Gardens** - 200-seat outdoor amphitheater for larger productions
- **Black Box Theatre** - 55-seat intimate indoor space

Their mission is to produce bold, re-imagined, entertaining, and accessible interpretations of Shakespeare and classical works, while promoting artistic excellence and cultivating the widest possible audience.

## History

**Established in 1985** as Oklahoma Shakespeare in the Park, the company has been a community leader in classical educational outreach for nearly 40 years. Since its founding, Oklahoma Shakespeare has provided essential access to live classical theatre for students and adults across the state.

Notable milestones:
- 1985: Founded as Oklahoma Shakespeare in the Park
- 2020-present: Major renovations to both venues, maintained operations through pandemic
- Current: Annual audience of nearly 10,000 patrons, supporting 100+ jobs per season
- Mayor David Holt was a young company member in his youth, showing multi-generational community impact

## Programming & Shows

Year-round classical theatre programming:

**Main Stage Productions:**
- Shakespeare plays (Hamlet, Romeo & Juliet, A Midsummer Night's Dream, etc.)
- Classical works from other playwrights
- Oklahoma premieres of new works
- Indoor shows (Black Box, 55 seats)
- Outdoor summer productions (Shakespeare Gardens, 200 seats)

**Educational Outreach:**
- Student matinee performances
- Touring shows to schools across Oklahoma
- Young company training camps
- Artist-in-residence programs
- Workshops and classes

Ticket prices kept intentionally affordable through grant support. Multiple discount programs for youth and underserved communities.
