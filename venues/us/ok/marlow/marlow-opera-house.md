# Marlow Opera House (Duncan Little Theatre)

## Overview
Community theater venue operated by Duncan Little Theatre (DLT), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit theatrical organization made up of volunteers from across south central Oklahoma. Despite the "Marlow Opera House" name, this venue appears to be Duncan Little Theatre's main facility. DLT presents plays, musicals, children's theater, and community productions. Founded in 1981 (reformed after earlier AAUW activity in 1950s-60s). The organization owns buildings at 913 W. Main St. in Duncan and a set/prop facility on Willow.

## History
Duncan Little Theatre originated as an activity of the American Association of University Women (AAUW), performing plays in the 1950s and 1960s. After a period of inactivity, a group of citizens reformed Duncan Little Theatre in 1981, incorporating as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit.

**Milestones:**
- **1981**: Reformed and incorporated
- **1995**: Hosted Oklahoma Community Theatre Association (OCTA) State Festival
- **2001**: 
  - Hosted Regional Southwest America Association of Community Theatres festival
  - Celebrated 20th anniversary with revival of "Once Upon A Mattress" (DLT's first production)
- **2005**: Added Willow facility for set supplies and construction
- **2017**: Celebrated 35th anniversary with variety show highlighting plays and musicals from 1993-2016

DLT is a founding member of OCTA (Oklahoma Community Theatre Association), the oldest community theatre organization west of the Mississippi River. Many DLT veterans have been recognized in OCTA competitions and traveled nationwide to compete.
