## About

Producers' Club Theaters is a landmark Off-Off Broadway theater rental facility in New York City's Theater District, walking distance from Times Square. The venue operates as a rental facility for independent theater producers, offering fully equipped theaters that convert into HD screening rooms, a bar and lounge, and three studios. Open seven days a week from morning through late nights, the club provides flexible space for theater productions, sketch shows, acting workshops, podcasts, and screenings.

**Office hours**: Monday - Friday, 9am - 7pm

## History

**1920**: Building constructed; originally operated as a speakeasy patroned by Irish immigrants working on the subway system during the height of the Irish mafia's operations in Hell's Kitchen.

**1963**: Actor and comedian Budd Friedman opened a 50-seat coffee house called The Improvisation for Broadway performers to wind down after shows. The venue attracted stars like Judy Garland (with daughter Liza Minnelli), Bette Midler (with pianist Barry Manilow), and then-unknown Dustin Hoffman. The Improv became **the first venue to present live stand-up in a continuous format**, revolutionizing the art form and creating the template for all comedy clubs that followed. Some Seinfeld cold opens were filmed at The Improv, possibly in what is now the Royal Theater. Larry David workshopped stand-up at the Improv in the 1970s.

**1974**: Friedman opened The Hollywood Improv (still operating today).

**1981**: Budd and Silver Friedman divorced; Budd acquired the California Improvs, Silver acquired the New York Improv.

**1992**: New York Improv closed.

**December 2006**: Albanian brothers Alfred and Ernest Tollja purchased the playhouse and have managed it as Producers' Club Theaters ever since.

## Programming & Shows

The venue operates as a **rental facility** rather than producing its own shows. Independent producers rent the theaters for:
- Off-Off Broadway theater productions
- Sketch comedy shows
- Acting workshops
- Podcasts
- HD screenings
- Special events

The venue does not appear to maintain a regular season or house productions - scheduling is determined by rental bookings.

## Opendate Fit

**Score: 15/100 — Poor Fit**

Not a viable candidate for Opendate:
- **Rental model**: The venue rents space to producers; they don't sell tickets themselves
- **No ticketing operation**: Individual productions handle their own ticketing
- **Fragmented customer base**: Each renter is a separate entity with their own systems
- **No transaction volume**: The Tollja brothers collect rent, not ticket revenue

**Why the initial high score was misleading**: The venue has high Google ratings and is a respected NYC theater space, but the business model is fundamentally incompatible with a ticketing platform. Opendate targets venues that produce/promote events and sell tickets, not facilities that rent space to others.

**Potential alternative**: If Opendate wanted to pursue this market, they could offer a "white-label" solution for theater renters who need ticketing, but that would be selling to the *renters*, not to Producers' Club itself.
