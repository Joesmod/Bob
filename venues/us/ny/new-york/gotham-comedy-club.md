# Gotham Comedy Club

## Overview
Premier comedy club in Manhattan's Chelsea neighborhood. Established venue with 25+ years of history hosting top comedians. Intimate cabaret-style setting with full bar and pub food menu.

## History
Opened May 10, 1996, founded by Chris Mazzilli and Mike Reisman. Opening night featured Paul Provenza, Mike Royce, Sarah Silverman, and Dave Chappelle. Now owned and operated by brothers Chris and Steven Mazzilli (teamed up 2005). Has launched careers of major comedians including Jim Gaffigan and Amy Schumer, while serving as NYC home to Jerry Seinfeld, Sebastian Maniscalco, and Trevor Noah.

## Film & Television
- Jerry Seinfeld's documentary "Comedian" filmed at the club
- "Last Comic Standing" (Emmy nominated) - 4 seasons
- "Live at Gotham" for Comedy Central with hosts including Kevin Hart, Bill Burr, Jim Breuer, Lewis Black
- "The Jim Gaffigan Show" used the club as backdrop
- "Gotham Comedy Live" on AXS TV with hosts including Craig Robinson, Anthony Anderson, Pete Davidson, Michael Che
- 2019: Partnered with Next VR and Oculus for "Live in Gotham" - first stand-up series shot in VR (30+ countries)
- 2019: Converted to "The Copacabana" for Martin Scorsese's "The Irishman" featuring Robert De Niro, Joe Pesci
