# Croton Reservoir Tavern

## Overview
Modern American restaurant and bar near Bryant Park in Midtown Manhattan. Opened 2003, named after the historic Croton Distributing Reservoir that once stood where the NY Public Library and Bryant Park are now. Features 150 sq ft hand-painted mural of the reservoir, 50-foot oak bar, and private downstairs lounge. NOT a ticketed entertainment venue.

## History
Opened 2003, a few blocks from Times Square. Named in memory of the Croton Distributing Reservoir (1842-1890s) which once stood at 42nd Street and Fifth Avenue - a 4-acre man-made lake with 50-foot granite walls that supplied NYC's drinking water for most of the 19th century. Edgar Allan Poe enjoyed walking atop the reservoir walls.

The restaurant features a 150 sq ft hand-painted mural depicting the reservoir. Brass studded leather chairs, wrought iron railings, and leather banquette seating evoke turn-of-the-century New York.
