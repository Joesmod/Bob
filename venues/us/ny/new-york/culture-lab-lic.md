# Culture Lab LIC

## Overview
Nonprofit arts advocacy organization and multidisciplinary arts venue in Long Island City, Queens. Founded by artists in 2021, Culture Lab LIC serves as a community hub for visual arts, theater, dance, comedy, and live music. The venue operates as both a gallery space and performing arts center, hosting everything from free weekly jazz nights to ticketed theater productions and art exhibitions.

## History
Founded in 2021 by local artists in Long Island City, NY. Winner of BOAST: Best of Astoria & LIC 2025 in multiple categories: Best Museum, Best Organization or Cause to Contribute To, Best Performing Arts Venue, and Best Place for Live Music.

## Programming & Shows
- **Theater**: Full productions including Liberty Scrap (March 5-29), Weapons of Mass Seduction (June 11-21)
- **Live Music**: Sunset Jazz (free, every Friday at 8pm), single release parties, concerts
- **Comedy**: Nice One! Comedy shows
- **Dance/Burlesque**: The Cabaret Burlesque (May performances)
- **Visual Arts**: Rotating exhibitions including Modern Ink (Chinese contemporary art), community exhibitions
- **Community Events**: NYC Plant Market (monthly), SpeakEasier Language Exchange, documentary screenings
- **Residency Programs**: New Works Lab (annual performing arts residency program)
- **Frequency**: Multiple events per week, gallery open Thu-Sun
- **Ticket prices**: Mix of free/donation-based and ticketed events ($15-$35 typical for theater)

Gallery Hours: Thursday & Friday 5-9pm, Saturday & Sunday 2-9pm

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 60/100 — Worth Watching**

Culture Lab LIC is an active arts venue with diverse programming, but presents challenges as a potential client. As a nonprofit founded in 2021, they run many free/donation-based programs alongside ticketed events, which may limit ticketing revenue. They appear to use multiple platforms (Humanitix spotted, possibly others) rather than a single consistent ticketing system.

**Pros**: High community engagement, multiple weekly events, award-winning venue, strong local presence, diverse programming (theater, music, comedy, visual arts).

**Cons**: Nonprofit model with significant free programming, unclear ticketing volume, may have limited budget for platform migration, appears to use multiple ticketing solutions suggesting fragmented needs.

**Recommendation**: Monitor for growth. Good fit if they consolidate ticketing operations and increase paid programming.
