## History

The Peoples Improv Theater was founded by Ali Reza Farahnakian in 2002 in a small blackbox theater at 154 W 29th Street. After outgrowing its original home, The PIT expanded to 123 E 24th Street on December 31, 2010, opening the PIT Striker & PIT Underground theaters. In 2015, the original W29th St space was renamed The PIT Loft. In February 2021, the PIT announced closure of its Flatiron location, consolidating operations at The PIT Loft in Manhattan.

The historic 154 W 29th St building is over a century old, previously serving as a synagogue and later hosting church services before becoming a theater space.
