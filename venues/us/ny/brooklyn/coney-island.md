# Coney Island USA

## Overview
Nonprofit arts organization and multi-arts performance space operating out of 1208 Surf Avenue in Coney Island, Brooklyn. Founded in 1980, the organization operates a circus sideshow, museum, performance venue, and bar in a historic 1917 Spanish Revival building (landmarked in 2011). Small-capacity venue focused on alternative/fringe arts, burlesque, sideshow, and cultural preservation.

## History
Founded in 1980 by Costa Mantis, Jane Savitt-Tennen, and Dick D. Zigun. Took possession of 1208 Surf Avenue building (built 1917) in 1996 and purchased it with NYC funding in 2008. The building was originally a Childs Restaurant, then served as a jazz nightclub (The Bluebird Casino), classic sideshow (David Rosen's Wonderland Sideshow), and arcades. Building granted NYC landmark status in 2011, making it the oldest structure in Coney Island protected by law.

## Programming & Shows
- **Circus Sideshow**: Sideshows By The Seashore - live performances
- **Burlesque at the Beach**: Regular burlesque shows ($25 typical)
- **Cemetery Cinema**: Classic horror/cult film screenings ($10-$15)
- **Special Events**: Drag shows, wrestling, variety shows, art openings
- **Classes**: Clown School, Sideshow School, Film School
- **Major Events**: Annual Mermaid Parade, Miss Coney Island Pageant, Congress of Curious Peoples
- **Frequency**: 2-4 ticketed events per week
- **Ticket prices**: $10-$38 for most events
