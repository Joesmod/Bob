# Hawkinsville Opera House

## Overview
Historic 576-seat opera house in Hawkinsville, GA. Built in 1907 by noted Macon architect W.R. Gunn, the venue features perfect acoustics and a 75-foot stage. Listed on the National Historic Register since 1973. Underwent $1.7 million renovation in 2000 with state-of-the-art theatrical lighting, sound, rigging systems, and modern facilities. Owned by the citizens of Hawkinsville and operated by the nonprofit Hawkinsville-Pulaski County Arts Council.

## History
Constructed in 1907 at a cost of $16,470, originally dubbed "The City Auditorium." The architect guaranteed perfect acoustics and sightlines—and never had to return his $1,000 fee. During its first decades, it hosted many traveling and well-known acts as the premier entertainment destination in Pulaski County. Served various community purposes over the years, including as a temporary church and movie theater. Placed on National Historic Register in 1973. Major renovation completed in 2000 with modern systems while preserving historic character.
