Atlanta's flagship improv comedy theater in Midtown. Nonprofit organization offering weekly improv shows, classes, workshops, and camps for all ages. Over 50 company members serve as performers, crew, and instructors. Shows run five times per week.

## History

Founded in 1993-1994 by David Webster, who trained with improv troupes in Chicago including The Second City. After six months living on the island of Kauai where he designed the vision for Whole World, Webster moved to Atlanta in 1993 and began teaching his style of emotional, experiential, long-form improv.

The group first performed at Eddie's Attic in Decatur, Georgia. In 1995, Webster secured Whole World's present location at 1216 Spring St. By 1999, the company expanded into television comedy, selling a show to Turner South. Webster wrote and directed a piece for TBS that won two Regional Emmy Awards, eventually sold to Castle Rock Entertainment.

David Webster served as artistic director until 2004. The theater has continued for over 30 years, becoming a landmark in Atlanta's comedy scene. Alumni include acclaimed actors, voice-over artists, puppeteers, writers, directors, and producers for TV and film.
