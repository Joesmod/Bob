Georgia's official State Theatre and a National Historic Landmark. Professional regional theatre company now in its 154th season, producing 10-15 productions annually across three different series. Attracts 112,000 annual admissions — Georgia's 2nd largest audience for live theatre.

## History

Built in 1871, the Springer Opera House has served as an engine for capital investment, job creation, community dialogue, education, and cultural enrichment in Columbus for over 150 years. The theatre survived numerous threats to its existence thanks to dedicated patrons and donors.

The building is a painstakingly preserved National Historic Landmark encompassing three floors and 150,000 square feet of program space covering most of a city block. Original features include heart-pine flooring, 88 historic heart-pine windows, historic Bradbury-and-Bradbury wallpapers, and ornamental ironwork.

Notable for surviving as a continuously operating theatre for over 150 years, making it one of the oldest theatres in America still in use.

## Education Programs

**Massive education operation serving 22,000+ students and 500 teachers annually:**

- **Springer Theatre Academy**: Training ground for ages 5-18, character-building through stage skills
- **Theatre for the Very Young**: Literacy, empathy, imagination for youngest audiences
- **Arts in Education**: 15,000 students attend daytime performances, workshops, tours (free buses via ArtServe)
- **First Act**: Free children's theatre program on conflict resolution, kindness, resilience
- **PAIR** (Professional Arts Integration Program): Unique program training 500+ teachers/year to integrate arts into curricula; only program of its kind in the country, partnered with Georgia Dept of Education

- Board approval

- Full CRM/donor database migration

- Likely 12-18 month evaluation and migration timeline

**Better target**: Smaller independent venues. Springer is established institutional theatre, not an Opendate sweet spot.
