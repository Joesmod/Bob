Historic theatre within LSU's Music & Dramatic Arts building. Hosts LSU School of Theatre and Turner-Fischer Center for Opera performances.

## History

The Music & Dramatic Arts building recently underwent a 4.5-year, $22 million renovation that included restoration of the historic Claude L. Shaver Theatre and creation of a new black-box Studio Theatre. These improvements have positioned CMDA to keep pace with and surpass other regional institutions.

## Facilities

**LSU CMDA operates multiple performance venues:**
- **Shaver Theatre** (350 capacity) - Main theatre for School of Theatre and Opera
- **Studio Theatre** - Black box space for Lab Season productions
- **School of Music Recital Hall** (250 capacity)
- **Union Theater** (1,269 capacity) - with Meyers Constellation acoustic system
- **Reilly Theatre/Swine Palace** - State-of-the-art facility (opened 2000)
- **Tiger Band Hall** - Rehearsal/performance space
- **Digital Media Center Theatre** - CCT/DMAE performances

## Programming

Primarily **LSU academic productions**:
- School of Theatre mainstage season
- Turner-Fischer Center for Opera productions
- Student showcases and recitals
- Lab Season productions
- Some events free and open to public

## Opendate Fit

**Score: 10/100 — Not a Target / Academic Theatre**

**Major Challenges:**
- **State university academic facility**: Part of LSU College of Music & Dramatic Arts
- **Educational purpose**: Primarily serves LSU students, faculty, and academic programs
- **Not a ticketed venue**: Most events are student productions, recitals, and showcases
- **Free/low-cost admission**: Many performances free to public (educational mission)
- **No commercial ticketing operation**: Not selling tickets to general public for profit
- **State institution**: Any platform decisions go through LSU IT, procurement, state compliance

**Reality Check:**
This is an academic theatre for LSU student productions and educational programs. It's not a commercial venue. The School of Theatre uses it for:
- Class productions (acting, directing, design students)
- Opera program showcases
- Student recitals and performances
- Educational workshops

**Not an Opendate target:** Academic theatres serve educational missions, not commercial entertainment. They don't need robust ticketing platforms — they need room reservations and occasional free event registration.

**Better targets:** Commercial venues selling tickets to the public for profit — NOT university academic theatres.
