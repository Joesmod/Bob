# Howells Opera House

## Overview
Historic 1907 opera house in Oakley, ID operated by the Oakley Valley Arts Council (non-profit). Community theater producing musicals and theatrical productions year-round including an annual Christmas concert. Volunteer-driven organization serving the Snake River plains of South Central Idaho.

## History
Built in 1907 by Judge Benjamin P. Howells for $22,000. Originally the most luxurious theater between Salt Lake City and Boise. In 1929, acquired by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and converted to the Oakley Playhouse movie theater. By the 1970s, faced threat of demolition. Community rallied to form the Oakley Valley Arts Council, purchased the building, and completed extensive restoration. Now serves as vibrant performing arts hub.
