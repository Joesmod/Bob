# Kenworthy Performing Arts Centre

## Overview
Historic 270-seat art-house cinema and performing arts venue in downtown Moscow, Idaho. Originally opened in 1926, the Kenworthy serves as the region's premier independent cinema while also hosting live music, comedy, theater, and community events. Recently completed renovation with Art-Deco chairs, custom carpet, and modern amenities.

## History
- **1908**: Building operated as The Crystal Theatre, Moscow's opera house
- **1926**: Milburn Kenworthy purchases and renames it Kenworthy Theatre; opened to public January 4, 1926
- **1927**: Purchased Robert Morton theatre pipe organ to accompany silent films (now at University of Idaho Auditorium)
- **1928**: Enlarged by 24 feet to the south; stage used for vaudeville and dramatic productions
- **1929**: Began showing "talking pictures"
- **1949**: Remodeled with terracotta tile façade and enlarged marquee
- **Late 1980s**: Leased after Kenworthy family operation
- **2000**: Gifted to Kenworthy Performing Arts Centre, Inc. (nonprofit)
- **2002**: Placed on National Register of Historic Places
- **Recent**: Chair Campaign completed—270 ergonomic Art-Deco chairs, custom carpet, dimmable aisle lighting

## Programming & Shows
**Very active calendar with diverse programming:**

### Film Programming
- **Art-house cinema**: Independent, foreign, documentary films
- **Film Series**:
  - Cinema Classics (committee-curated classic films)
  - Centennial Series (iconic films from each Kenworthy decade)
  - Moscow Film Society (themed series like "Make Me Famous")
  - Frames of Reference (art in film, with Exhibition on Screen series)
  - Science on Screen® (films with scientist introductions)
  - Page to Screen (BookPeople collaboration)
  - Staff Picks
  - Oscars Recap (Academy Award nominees)
- **Special Events**: Dog-friendly screenings, Oscars Watch Party

### Live Events
- **Music**: Local and touring acts (Moscow Music & More Showcase, Rendezvous benefit concerts)
- **Theater**: Live productions and visiting companies
- **Comedy**: Stand-up and variety shows
- **Community Events**: Film festivals, fundraisers, private rentals
- **Opera**: Met Live in HD broadcasts

### Current Event Sample (March 2026)
- 20+ events per month
- Film Pass subscription available
- Multiple screenings daily (matinees and evening shows)

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 85/100 — Excellent Lead**

**Positives:**
- High event frequency (20+ events/month, year-round)
- Active online ticketing via Square (switchable platform)
- Diverse programming beyond just film (music, theater, comedy, community)
- Strong community nonprofit with engaged board
- Historic venue with recent capital improvements (invested in infrastructure)
- Subscription/membership model (Film Pass, donors) suggests sophisticated audience
- Private rental business adds revenue stream

**Why Opendate:**
The Kenworthy is actively selling tickets online for diverse events. Square works for basic e-commerce but lacks features for:
- Sophisticated film series/membership management
- Multi-event passes and subscriptions
- Donor integration (they have a donate page but it's separate from ticketing)
- Detailed analytics across film vs. live event audiences
- Reserved seating with seatmaps (valuable for 270-seat historic venue)

As a nonprofit arts center with active programming and recent renovations, they're ready for a platform that integrates ticketing, memberships, donations, and CRM. Strong candidate for Opendate.
