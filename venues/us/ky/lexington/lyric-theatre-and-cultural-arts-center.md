# Lyric Theatre & Cultural Arts Center

## Overview

Historic cultural center in Lexington's East End, now operating as "Lyric Live." One of the last remaining Chitlin Circuit venues, the Lyric is dedicated to uplifting and empowering the community through arts, education, and cultural programs celebrating Black heritage and culture. The venue offers performances, exhibitions, educational activities, and community events.

## History

The Lyric stands as a cultural landmark and one of the last remaining Chitlin Circuit venues — historic theaters that hosted African American performers during segregation. For generations, the Lyric has served as a gathering place and cultural hub in Lexington's East End. Today, the organization continues its legacy by offering inspiring performances, impactful programs, vibrant events, and a welcoming space for everyone, with a focus on preserving Black heritage and celebrating African American culture.

## Programming & Shows

**2026 Monthly Themes** (comprehensive cultural programming):

- **February**: "Roots of Resistance" (Harriet Tubman, Underground Railroad)

- **April**: "The Great Migration and the Making of America"
- **May**: "Black Jockeys, Barns, and Brilliance" (Kentucky equestrian history)
- **June**: "Freedom to Express: Juneteenth & Beyond"
- **July**: "Fly Boys and Freedom Fighters" (Black military service)
- **August**: "From Blues to Hip-Hop" (music history)
- **September**: "Education as Liberation" (HBCUs, educators)
- **October**: "Black Futures: Innovation and Imagination" (Afrofuturism, STEM)
- **November**: "Faith, Food, and Family" (traditions, gospel, food heritage)
- **December**: "Celebrate US" (Kwanzaa, year in review)

**Regular Programs**:
