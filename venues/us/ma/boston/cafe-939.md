## About

The Red Room at Cafe 939 is Berklee College of Music's premier all-ages concert venue. With a 200-person standing room capacity, it serves as an intimate space for discovering emerging talent across all genres — from rock and jazz to folk, hip-hop, bluegrass, and avant-garde.

The venue has hosted acclaimed artists including Young the Giant, Hozier, Tyler Childers, Snarky Puppy, and Tori Kelly. The Red Room focuses on both showcasing music and developing artists, featuring top-quality sound engineering with Berklee students working alongside industry professionals.

The venue does not sell alcohol. Unless otherwise specified, all shows are standing room only.

## History

Part of Berklee College of Music's longstanding commitment to live music performance and artist development. The Red Room has become a key discovery venue in Boston's music scene, helping launch careers of artists who later played major venues.

## Programming & Shows

Diverse programming across genres:

- **Fresh Prospects** - Free concert series featuring emerging artists curated by Berklee students
- **Nocturnia** - Electronic Music Club's flagship DJ and visual art events (free)
- **Bass Bash** - Special clinics/events with legendary musicians (Lee Sklar, Victor Wooten, Joe Dart)
- **Faculty Artist Clinics** - Educational performances by Berklee faculty
- **Touring artists** - Mix of indie, rock, folk, hip-hop, electronic acts
- **Student showcases** - Berklee student performances

Most shows are **free admission**, though some ticketed events occur. Shows typically run multiple nights per week.
