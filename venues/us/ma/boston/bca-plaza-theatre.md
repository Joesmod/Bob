# BCA Plaza Theatre

## Overview

Boston Center for the Arts (BCA) is an arts incubator and cultural center supporting working artists. The Plaza Theatres are part of the BCA campus at 539 Tremont Street in Boston's South End. The facility includes multiple venues: Plaza Theatres, the Cyclorama, Mills Gallery, and the separate Calderwood Pavilion.

## History

Opened to the public in 1970, Boston Center for the Arts has for five decades engaged creative community for public good. Originally rooted in visual arts and theatre, BCA now encompasses all forms and disciplines practiced by working artists across Boston. The organization provides residencies and programming for artists working across all disciplines, serving as an epicenter for experimentation and artistic risk-taking.
