The Calderwood Pavilion is a performance venue within the Boston Center for the Arts (BCA) complex in Boston's historic South End. BCA supports working artists to create, perform, and exhibit new works; develops new audiences; and connects the arts to community.

## History

Boston Center for the Arts opened to the public in 1970 and has for five decades engaged creative community for public good. While physically located in the historic South End, BCA touches every part of Boston's cultural ecosystem.

BCA has supported thousands of individual artists, small organizations, and performing arts companies who add depth to Boston's arts ethos. Through residencies and programming, BCA serves as an epicenter for artists working across all disciplines, catalyzing careers by providing fertile ground for experimentation and artistic risk-taking.

The artistic programs at BCA evolved from roots in visual arts and theatre to encompass all forms and disciplines practiced by working artists across Boston. The Calderwood Pavilion serves as one of BCA's key performance venues.

**Land Acknowledgment**: BCA recognizes that it works, learns, and creates on the ancestral land and unceded territory of the Wampanoag, Nipmuc, and Massachusett People.
