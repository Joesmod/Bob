# Hatch Memorial Shell

## Overview

The Hatch Memorial Shell (commonly called the "Hatch Shell") is Boston's iconic outdoor amphitheater on the Charles River Esplanade. Built in 1941, it's the city's epicenter for large outdoor performances and free concerts. The venue is best known for hosting the Boston Pops Orchestra's Fourth of July concert.

## History

The Music Oval on the Esplanade was first established in 1929 when Arthur Fiedler and the Boston Pops performed their first outdoor concert. The current shell was constructed in 1941 with a $300,000 trust donated by benefactor Maria Hatch to build a memorial for her late brother, Edward.

The Esplanade itself was expanded beginning in 1928, aided by a one million dollar donation from Helen Osbourne Storrow in memory of her husband James. The Hatch Shell has been a Boston landmark for over 80 years, hosting classical, jazz, blues, rock and roll, and local performances from spring through fall.

The most notable event is the annual July 4th Boston Pops concert, conducted by Keith Lockhart, featuring Tchaikovsky's 1812 Overture with fireworks over the Charles River.
