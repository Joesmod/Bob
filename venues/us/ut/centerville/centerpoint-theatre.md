62,000 square foot multi-venue performing arts facility in Centerville, Utah. Houses a main stage proscenium theater, intimate black box theater, rehearsal halls, dressing rooms, and set/costume construction areas. Serves over 100,000 patrons annually with theatrical performances, musicals, and community programs.

## History

Founded in 1990 as Pages Lane Theatre by Joan and Ralph Rodgers, Beverly and Blaine Olsen, and Margo and David Beecher. Originally operated as a 287-seat theater-in-the-round at 292 East Pages Lane. The first production was *Cheaper by the Dozen* on November 16, 1990.

Renamed Rodgers Memorial Theatre in 1996 in honor of founder Ralph Rodgers who died that year. Underwent renovations to add a proscenium stage space.

In 2011, the organization moved to its current state-of-the-art facility at the Davis Center for the Performing Arts and was renamed CenterPoint Legacy Theatre. Ground was broken in June 2009 under the leadership of Centerville Mayor Ronald G. Russell and theatre leaders Glenn McKay, Scott VanDyke, Alan Mortensen, Kevin Ellis, and Bill Davies.

## Programming & Shows

CenterPoint produces theatrical performances and musicals throughout the year. The facility supports:

- **Main Stage Productions**: Full-scale musicals and plays in the proscenium theater
- **Black Box Shows**: Intimate performances in smaller venue
- **Youth Programs**: CenterPoint Academy for youth performers
- **Community Outreach**: Friend to Friend program for individuals with special needs, Troubadour program for elementary students, BRAVO! program for secondary school students
- **Annual Performers**: Over 500 local performers participate each year

The theater emphasizes stories representing diverse cultures and backgrounds within the community.
