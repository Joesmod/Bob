# Brown Grand Theatre

## Overview
Historic 750-capacity opera house in Concordia, Kansas. Built in 1907, fully restored in 1980, this National Historic Site serves as a community-based performing arts center presenting concerts, theater, films, graduations, and cultural events across North Central Kansas.

## History
**Construction (1906-1907):** Colonel Napoleon Bonaparte Brown announced plans in November 1905 to build Concordia a fully outfitted opera house. Renowned Kansas City theater architect Carl Boiler designed the $40,000 Renaissance-style structure using local labor, native limestone, and locally fired bricks. The 60-foot-high, 120-foot-long building opened September 17, 1907 with a production of *The Vanderbilt Cup*.

**Early Years (1907-1925):** Hailed as "the most elegant theatre between Kansas City and Denver," the Brown Grand hosted vaudeville, opera, dance, and theater. Notable performers included Madame Ernestine Schumann-Heink, Laurette Taylor, Ruth St. Denis, and Martha Graham.

**Film Era (1925-1974):** Purchased by Concordia Amusement Company in 1925 and converted to a movie house. Underwent multiple remodelings for talkies, wide-screen, and air-conditioning. Last film screened September 10, 1974.

**Restoration (1975-1980):** Listed on National Historic Register (1973). Community fundraising campaign "Lend a Hand to the Brown Grand" raised nearly $250,000. Phase I (1976-1978): exterior restoration. Phase II (1978-1980): interior restoration including plaster molding repair, gold leaf stenciling, original color scheme restoration (white, green, gold), and installation of donated theater seats from Bethany College. The famous "Napoleon at Austerlitz" drop curtain was repainted by Twin City Scenic Company. Reopened with a restaging of *The Vanderbilt Cup* featuring three attendees from the original 1907 opening.

**Today:** Operated by Brown Grand Opera House Inc. as a cultural hub for North Central Kansas.

## Programming & Shows
- **Classic Movie Series**: Free screenings with donation encouragement (3 films per season: *Cat on a Hot Tin Roof*, *Vertigo*, *Citizen Kane*)
- **Live Concerts**: Touring acts (The Swing Dolls, Reza - Edge of Illusion Tour)
- **Theater Productions**: Missoula Children's Theatre residencies, community productions
- **Talent Competition**: Annual "Concordia's Got Talent" with $500+ in prizes
- **Youth Programs**: Lovewell Institute for the Creative Arts (one-week musical creation intensive)
- **Community Events**: Service dog graduations (quarterly), church services (Palm Sunday), dance recitals, school concerts
- **Educational**: Historic tours by appointment

**Ticket Prices:**
- Classic Movies: Free (donations encouraged)
- The Swing Dolls: $20-$40
- Reza Magic Show: $25-$35
- Concordia's Got Talent Finals: $15 (one division) / $25 (both divisions)
- Missoula Children's Theatre: $10/adult, free for kids under 10
- Many community events: Free

**Frequency**: 2-4 events per month, year-round programming

## Facilities
- **Capacity**: Approximately 750 seats (main floor + balcony)
- **Stage**: Full proscenium with original pin rail system (20 feet above stage)
- **Dressing Rooms**: 5 modernized dressing rooms with lighting and plumbing
- **Special Features**: 
  - Historic "Napoleon at Austerlitz" drop curtain
  - Box seats
  - Ladies Parlor (restored period room)
  - Orchestra pit
  - Modern lighting system (ceiling cove + stage lighting)
  - Green velvet seating and draperies
  - Brass fixtures throughout
- **Accessibility**: Main floor handicapped accessible via west entrance ramp; no elevator to balcony. Wheelchair seating at back of main floor plus swing-away arm seats.
