# Merrimans' Playhouse

## Overview
Intimate jazz listening room and nonprofit 501(c)(3) arts organization in downtown South Bend, IN. Operates as a "Sonic Art Gallery" focused on high-level improvised jazz performance in an acoustically designed listening environment. Limited seating, artist-driven programming.

## History
Founded in 2006 by Stephen and Mary Merriman. Initially presented concerts at South Bend Century Center's Recital Hall (2006-2011), then opened their own venue at 1211 Mishawaka Avenue (2011-2020). Incorporated as nonprofit in 2017. Moved to current location at Commerce Center building on Colfax Ave in East Bank Village in 2020, reopening July 29, 2021 during the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Programming & Shows
- **Jazz Performance Series**: Regional, national, and international touring jazz musicians
- **Home Grown Jazz Series**: Local area jazz ensembles (6th year)
- **Student Jazz Performance Series**: College and high school jazz groups
- **Chamber Arts Series**: Chamber jazz, classical, traditional music with educational elements
- **Jazz Open Sessions**: Every Tuesday, open to local musicians and students
- **Summer Jazz Series**: Four concerts at University of Notre Dame Raclin Murphy Museum (partnership since 2015)
- **Radio Partnerships**: "The Sauce Live at Merrimans' Playhouse" on WVPE 88.1 FM (local NPR), plus formerly hosted "This is the Gig" on WETF 105.7 FM
- **Ticket prices**: $6-$10 students, $12-$20 general admission
- **Schedule**: Concerts typically start at 7:30pm, end around 9pm (one-set concerts)
- Doors open at 7:00pm
