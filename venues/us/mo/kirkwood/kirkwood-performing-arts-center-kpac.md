# Kirkwood Performing Arts Center (KPAC)

## Overview

State-of-the-art performing arts facility in downtown Kirkwood, Missouri. Multi-venue complex hosting theater productions, concerts, and private events. Operated by the City of Kirkwood in partnership with STAGES (resident theater company) and other regional arts organizations.

## Venues

- **Ross Family Theatre**: 530-seat auditorium with proscenium stage
- **Strauss Black Box Theatre**: 375 capacity flexible space
- **Event Space**: 2,000 sq ft, 275 capacity (connected to Black Box)
- **Kent Family Main Lobby**: 3,800 sq ft
- **Kent Family 2nd Floor Lobby**: 1,900 sq ft
- **Event Lawn**: 3,500 sq ft outdoor space
- **Support spaces**: Dressing rooms, green rooms, wardrobe room, catering kitchen, concession stations

## History

Modern facility opened to serve Kirkwood's arts community. Built to provide a premier venue for local and regional arts organizations to showcase their work.

## Programming & Shows

- **KPAC Presents**: Coffee Concerts, Strauss Studio Series (classical to contemporary)
- **STAGES**: In-residence theater company presenting multiple productions per season
- **Kirkwood Parks & Recreation**: Community programming
- **Partner organizations**: Regional arts organizations present ticketed performances
- **Gallery Program**: Four seasonal art exhibitions per year by Kirkwood Arts Commission
- **Genres**: Theater, classical music, contemporary music, dance, comedy
- **Private events**: Corporate parties, weddings, meetings (up to 1,000 capacity for events)

## Opendate Fit

**Score: 60/100 — Medium Priority**

Multi-venue arts center with active programming and private event business. Currently using MetroTix for ticketing. The facility hosts multiple resident and partner organizations, which creates complexity but also opportunity. City-owned operation means municipal government decision-making. Strong facility rental business (up to 1,000 capacity for events) suggests they need robust event management alongside ticketing. The partnership model with STAGES and other organizations means any ticketing switch would need to serve multiple constituents. Accessible facility management team with clear contacts. Could be a good fit if Opendate can serve both their presenting shows and their private event business. MetroTix is a regional player, so switching costs may be moderate.
