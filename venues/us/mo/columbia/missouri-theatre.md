# Missouri Theatre

## Overview

Historic performing arts theater on the National Register of Historic Places. Built in 1928, Missouri Theatre is one of central Missouri's only remaining pre-Depression era movie palaces and vaudeville stages. Operated by Mizzou Arts (University of Missouri's arts program), hosting the University Concert Series.

## History

Built in 1928 as a movie palace and vaudeville stage. The theater's interior features ornate baroque and rococo style of the Louis XIV and XV periods. Designed by the Boller Brothers Architects of Kansas City, modeled after the Paris Opera House. Much of the original detailing survives, including Belgian marble wainscoting, plaster reliefs, stained glass art panels under the balcony, and an 1,800-pound Italian chandelier featuring crystal prisms and etched panels.
