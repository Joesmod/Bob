# Southern Theatre

## Overview
Historic 890-seat theater in downtown Columbus, Ohio, operated by CAPA (Columbus Association for the Performing Arts). Opened in 1896 as part of a performance space and hotel complex. Oldest surviving theater in central Ohio and one of the oldest in the state. Now presents a diverse range of performing arts including theater, dance, music, comedy, and family shows.

## History
Opened in 1896 on the corner of High and Main Streets in Columbus. Originally designed for theatrical touring productions, later accommodated silent films, vaudeville, motion pictures, dance, country music, and community events. Now managed by CAPA, a major regional nonprofit arts presenter founded in 1969. CAPA also operates the Ohio Theatre, Lincoln Theatre, and Riffe Center theaters in Columbus, plus the Shubert Theatre in New Haven, CT.
