# Firehall Arts Centre

## Overview
Historic performing arts venue in Vancouver's Downtown Eastside. Converted fire station (built 1906, converted to theatre 1982) that produces and presents theatre and contemporary dance. One of Canada's most respected cultural hubs, focusing on Indigenous and culturally diverse artists.

## History
Opened February 25, 1982 with a performance by Axis Theatre. Firehall Theatre Society incorporated in 1983. In 1985, established itself as one of the first theatre companies to involve Indigenous and culturally diverse artists. Built in 1906 as one of Vancouver's first fire stations, operated until mid-1970s. Over 40 years of operation as an arts venue.

## Programming & Shows
- **Disciplines**: Theatre, contemporary dance, visual arts
- **Focus**: Canadian work highlighting multiculturalism, Indigenous and culturally diverse artists
- **Notable**: Dancing on the Edge Festival (Canada's longest-running dance festival, spun off into own society in 1988)
- **Model**: Mix of productions, presentations, exhibitions
- **Venue Rentals**: Also offers theatre and studio rentals to community
