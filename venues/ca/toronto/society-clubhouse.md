# Society Clubhouse

## Overview
Women-owned, community-focused event space in Toronto's West End (College Street & Dovercourt Road). 3,400 sq ft multi-purpose venue that includes a taproom, artisan market, bottle shop, and event rental space. Can accommodate up to 175 guests. Home to Society of Beer Drinking Ladies' curated bottle shop.

## History
Established as a hybrid community space in Toronto's Dufferin Grove neighborhood. Women-owned business creating an inclusive community hub combining retail, social space, and event rentals.

## Opendate Fit
**Score: 10/100 — NOT A FIT**

**This is an event rental space + taproom, not a live entertainment venue.**

Society Clubhouse operates as:
- **Event rental facility** (private bookings for parties, corporate events, weddings)
- **Taproom/bar** (walk-in beer/wine sales and on-premise consumption)
- **Retail shop** (curated bottle shop, artisan market, merch)
- **Community space** (neighborhood hangout)

They do NOT:
- Present ticketed public performances
- Host concert series
- Sell admission tickets
- Program scheduled entertainment

**Why this was flagged**: "Event space" terminology likely triggered venue classification. However, event rental spaces serve private functions (weddings, corporate events, parties) and don't need public ticketing systems.

**Rental model vs. ticketing model**: Event venues that do private rentals operate on contracts and deposits, not ticketed admissions. Their revenue comes from rental fees, catering, and bar minimums - not ticket sales.

**Recommendation**: Remove from lead pipeline. This business model doesn't align with Opendate's ticketing platform. If they occasionally host a community event with ticket sales, volume would be negligible (maybe 1-2 events/year).

**Venue Type**: Should be classified as "Event Rental Space/Bar" not "Venue" in the database.
