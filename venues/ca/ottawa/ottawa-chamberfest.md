# Ottawa Chamberfest

## Overview
One of Canada's leading chamber music organizations, presenting classical and contemporary chamber music through an acclaimed summer festival and year-round concert series. Founded in 1994, Chamberfest has grown into one of the world's largest and most respected chamber music festivals, presenting hundreds of artists annually across multiple venues in Ottawa.

## History
Founded in 1994 as the Ottawa International Chamber Music Festival with 22 concerts, it was an immediate success. Over three decades, it has expanded into a year-round arts organization with three pillars: summer festival, concert series, and community engagement/education programs. Gryphon Trio cellist Roman Borys served as Artistic and Executive Director from 2007-2020. Since 2020, Carissa Klopoushak has served as Artistic Director, with Cate Proctor appointed Executive Director in 2025.

## Programming & Shows
- **Genres**: Chamber music (classical, contemporary, cross-genre collaborations)
- **Programming**: Summer festival (75+ performances over 2+ weeks across 12 venues) + year-round Concert Series (Marina Kun Concert Series)
- **Venues Used**: Carleton Dominion-Chalmers Centre, Beechwood Cemetery Sacred Space, City Hall lawn, museums, embassies, heritage sites
- **Notable Artists**: James Ehnes, Angela Hewitt, Marc-André Hamelin, Wallis Giunta, Emerson String Quartet, Danish String Quartet, Isabel Bayrakdarian, Sir András Schiff
- **Ticket prices**: Varies by performance; student/community discount codes available (GOODVIBRATO)
- **Current Season**: 2025-26 Marina Kun Concert Series running through summer 2026
