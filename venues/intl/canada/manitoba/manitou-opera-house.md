## About

The Manitou Opera House is a beautifully restored 1930s theatre serving as a multi-generational community hub in Manitoba's Pembina Valley. The venue embraces inclusivity, nurtures educational experiences, and celebrates artistic diversity through music, theatre, dance, visual arts, and community events.

Built in the Arts and Crafts style following a 1930 fire that destroyed the original 1905 building, the Opera House underwent a major $1.3 million accessibility renovation completed in 2016. Today it features a proscenium stage with excellent acoustics, state-of-the-art sound and lighting systems, and flexible event spaces.

The venue is managed by dedicated volunteers of the Manitou Opera House Foundation Board with support from "Friends of the Opera House" volunteers. Programming includes concerts, theatrical productions, art exhibitions, community events, festivals, and private rentals.

## History

**1878-1885**: Early community halls and gathering places on second floors of businesses. The Loyal Orange Lodge built the first dedicated Town Hall around 1885.

**1905**: Manitou Town Council (Mayor Wes McClung) built a new community hall at 325 Main Street. The building was championed by Wes and Nellie McClung, known for promoting the arts including public speaking clubs, band programs, reading rooms, and concerts.

**1908**: Within weeks of opening, confusion arose between the old and new town halls. The new building was renamed "Manitou Opera House" and has kept that name for over 100 years. The venue hosted Chautauqua Festivals and readings by local novelist and social activist Nellie McClung.

**January 16, 1930**: Fire destroyed the original building. Fireman Robert A. J. Brown died fighting the blaze and was honored at the opening ceremonies of the new building.

**Summer 1930**: The current Arts and Crafts style building was constructed by Blackburn Construction for $15,000, built on the foundation of the previous structure. Features included:
- Auditorium on upper floors
- Town constable offices in basement with vault and two prisoner cells

**1930s-1990s**: Hosted musical comedies, operettas, memorial services, bazaars, ghost shows, band concerts, arts festivals, school workshops, Hospital Auxiliary Teas, Neighbour Nites, dinner theatres, and drama performances. Later housed government offices and a clothing factory as entertainment bookings declined.

**1993**: Local entertainers (Coffeehouse Musicians, Borderline Singers, Candlewick Players) spearheaded a revival and took over management. First coffeehouse held by Coffeehouse Musicians.

**1995**: Geothermal heating and cooling system installed during restoration.

**1996**: Manitou Opera House Foundation established.

**2015-2016**: Major $1.3 million Accessibility Project:
- Accessible addition with adequate main floor washrooms
- Berm entrance for direct performance level access
- Major backstage area expansion
- New facility seamlessly integrated with original building
- Grand opening: April 23, 2016

**Present**: Fully operational as multi-purpose community arts hub with active programming.

**Regular Programming:**
- **Music**: Concerts, coffeehouses, singer performances, community bands
- **Theatre**: Dinner theatre, dramas, community productions, youth musicals
- **Visual Arts**: Art exhibitions, traveling exhibitions in multiple gallery spaces
- **Dance**: Dance lessons, performances, swing dance nights
- **Community Events**: Remembrance Day services, Christmas Ministerial Concert, craft sales, polling station
- **Festivals**: Honey, Garlic & Maple Syrup Festival concert, Pembina Valley Music Festival (piano, speech arts, vocal, band)
- **Fundraisers**: Winter coffeehouses benefiting local organizations

**Stakeholder Programming:**
- **Coffeehouse Musicians**: Regular coffeehouses with local artists and refreshments
- **Borderline Singers**: Choral concerts
- **Candlewick Players**: Theatre productions including junior and senior high school musicals

**Rental Opportunities:** Available for weddings, family gatherings, meetings, vendor markets
